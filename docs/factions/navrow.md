# Navrow

#### [NAV-roh]

!!! quote ""
    *"If it floats, we'll sail it. If it sells, we'll move it. And if it breathes, it better stay out of our waters. The Navtog may have fleets, but we have the shadows. And the shadows are faster, quieter, and they cut deeper."*

    *~Fekla Belova*

Navrow is a decentralized network of smugglers, pirates, and traders dominating the black-market seas of Dheghom. They deal in all goods, including contraband, quasi-legal wares, and luxury items while employing secrecy and speed to outmaneuver rivals and authorities alike. Their operations rely on hidden warehouses, covert supply chains, and a loose hierarchy led by the enigmatic Shadow Admiral. Regional captains and smugglers oversee their territories with significant autonomy, thriving on adaptability and opportunism.

Navrow emerged as independent smugglers banded together in response to centralized trade powers like The Navtog. Facing increased policing of the seas, these smugglers found it necessary to join forces, even haphazardly, to protect their interests. This loose alliance evolved into a sophisticated network, leveraging chaos and distrust of centralized authorities to expand their reach. Today, Navrow's influence stretches across the Long Deep and into the heartlands of Dheghom's major nations, hunting as much as they are hunted.

### Navrow on Other Factions

Navrow exploits cracks in power structures, seeing factions as opportunities to exploit, rivals to undermine, or temporary allies to support their shifting goals. Their reliance on covert operations and adaptability keeps relationships fluid and unpredictable.

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**Celtumpra.** The Navrow unknowingly move magical goods they believe to be either mundane or contraband. They view these secretive clients as nothing more than eccentric merchants who pay well and on time.

**Colderyn.** The Colderyn hire Navrow crews to smuggle goods and bypass Navtog tariffs. However, a Navrow captain will not hesitate to acquire additional Colderyn assets from any non-Navrow vessel if the profit outweighs the risk.

**Mustring.** They frequently partner with the Mustring to combine their maritime power with the syndicate's land-based networks. They are also, however, direct rivals for control of coastal black markets and many captains will betray a deal if a more profitable opportunity arises.

**The Navtog.** The Navtog are the primary threat of every Navrow captain, and their military fleets are the greatest threat on the open sea. A successful raid on a Navtog vessel is considered the ultimate prize and a badge of honor among pirate crews.

**The Osprey.** The Osprey never intentionally reveal themselves when offering contracts to Navrow, but they pay well and are rarely refused. The Navrow are rarely aware of who their benefactor is and treat them as any other client.

**The Ironpenny Poke.** The Poke's need for raw materials makes them a valuable client for Navrow smugglers, while also acting as trading partners that charter ships to avoid tariffs. However, non-Navrow vessels, much like coastal factories and warehouses, are frequently targets for opportunistic raids.

**Zaharmak.** Zaharmak cells are desperate, often broke clients for smuggled arms. Navrow captains view them as unreliable, but will take their money if the risk is low and will sell them out to the authorities if the bounty is higher.

### Navrow Roles

Authority in Navrow comes from cunning, strength, and seizing opportunity. Corsairs, Reavers, and Harbor Lords steer the faction's direction through the Council of Tides, while Operatives like Raiders and Quartermasters enforce their will. Behind the scenes, Support roles like Navigators and Boatswains keep Navrow's ships ready and its routes hidden.

#### Leaders

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**Shadow Admiral.** The elusive figurehead of Navrow, uniting the faction in crises and dictating strategy from the shadows.

**Corsairs.** Captains commanding fleets and leading naval assaults across contested waters and members of The Council of Tides.

**Reavers.** Captains directing raids, smuggling operations, and maintaining control over their crew and members of The Council of Tides.

**Harbor Lords.** Overseers of hidden ports and safe havens, wielding power through control of resources and members of The Council of Tides.

#### Operatives

**Brokers.** Operatives managing smuggling operations and facilitating the exchange of stolen goods and contraband.

**Hawks.** Scouts and spotters monitoring rival activity and identifying opportunities in key waters.

**Mates.** General crew members who carry out the captain's orders and participate in ship operations, raids, and combat.

**Powder Masters.** Experts in explosive weaponry, outfitting ships with superior firepower and crafting devastating naval traps.

**Quartermasters.** Enforcers responsible for discipline, leading boarding parties, and commanding captured vessels.

**Raiders.** Saboteurs specializing in disrupting rival trade routes, sinking ships, and destabilizing opposing factions.

#### Support

**Boatswains.** Engineers and builders responsible for maintaining and repairing Navrow ships.

**Navigators.** Specialists in secret routes and hidden ports, ensuring safe passage and evasion of rival forces.

### Navrow NPCs

Navrow's people live by instinct and opportunity, thriving where others see chaos. Captains command loyalty through charisma or fear, leading their crews with promises of riches or retribution. Smugglers and traders operate in moral gray areas, taking risks to stay ahead of rivals. Deckhands and informants gamble everything for survival, knowing that one mistake could cost them everything.

#### Major Players in Navrow

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**The Shadow Admiral.** The Shadow Admiral's genius lies not in naval strategy, but in social engineering. Their primary method of control is to ensure their captains are locked in perpetual, unresolvable blood feuds. They are an architect of grudges, secretly engineering situations where one captain's actions lead to the death of another's family, then delivering condolences with just enough falsified evidence to direct the bereaved captain's rage. This manufactured hatred keeps the crews vicious, motivated, and too consumed by infighting to ever unite against the Admiral, who remains the only stable point of power in the web of atrocities they designed.

**Bronagh Ui Aeda Odba.** Bronagh Ui Aeda Odba oversees a network of hidden ports in the Sea of Khun. Once a victim of The Navtog's slavers, her survival taught her not the value of freedom, but the profitability of treating people as cargo. She now traffics in human flesh, supplying work crews for illegal mining operations and selling press-ganged sailors to the highest bidder, using the same methods of branding and bloody discipline she once endured. Her vendetta against The Navtog is not one of justice, but of market competition.

**Kallistos Drakos.** Kallistos Drakos, Reaver Captain of the Bloodwake, is a feared name among the Baleros raiders. When the Navtog destroyed his clan, the few survivors were left to starve and turned to consuming their own dead to survive. This practice has since become a ritualized part of their raids. His crew follows him with unwavering devotion because they have all shared in the "Bloodwake Sacrament," and they now plunder ships not just for goods, but for fresh meat to sustain their voyages.

### Navrow Adventures

Navrow survives on speed and cunning, thriving where maritime control falters and loopholes abound. When you think about Navrow in your campaign, consider how their smuggling operations undercut trade monopolies, spread illicit goods, and create chaos that forces others to adapt. They act out of necessity, greed, and stubborn pride, but always with a focus on staying ahead of their enemies and the systems that seek to crush them.

Aligning with Navrow means embracing risk. A captain might hire the adventurers for a smuggling run, a raid on a rival ship, or to secure a black-market deal. Success brings rewards, but Navrow's enemies are legion, and failure often comes with violent consequences. Trust in Navrow is a scarce commodity, and adventurers will quickly learn that survival matters more than loyalty.

Navrow's strength as a faction lies in their unpredictability. Their lack of central authority means the party could encounter wildly different faces of Navrow, from a charismatic captain to a vengeful deckhand. Their smuggling operations and raids don't just destabilize economies, they also force other factions to adapt, creating opportunities for adventurers to intervene or profit.

Focus on the volatility of Navrow's network. A misstep by one crew could trigger a chain reaction, pulling the adventurers into rivalries and betrayals they didn't see coming. Navrow isn't a faction to defeat; they're a storm to survive.

!!! info "Faction Scenario: Navrow"
    **Primary Operation.** To profit financially, personally, or ecumenically. Navrow's strength and menace come from the diverse, personal ambitions of their individual captains. Each captain's primary objective is a reflection of their own greed, trauma, or desires with no regard for the cost to others.

    #### Methods
	___

	<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

    **Coastal Predation.** Establish temporary anchorages near vulnerable settlements to systematically bleed them dry. They raid for supplies and valuables, extort "protection" money from locals, and carry off villagers to be held for ransom or sold into slavery, leaving behind terrorized and ruined communities.

    **Slave Raiding.** People are a commodity to be taken and sold. They attack refugee vessels and poorly defended towns to capture able-bodied workers, women, and children. Captives are used for labor and personal gratification by the crew before being sold into the slave markets of more powerful nations.

    **Open-Sea Piracy.** Navrow attacks merchant vessels on the open sea, using superior seamanship and brute force. The fate of a captured crew depends on the captain's whim and needs; some are murdered to a man, while skilled sailors from other crews might be press-ganged into service.

    #### Complications
	___

    **Allies of Convenience.** Navrow captains forge alliances with any faction that can offer a payday. They will smuggle goods for The Poke and Colderyn to bypass Navtog tariffs one day, only to raid their coastal warehouses the next. They trade illegal goods with Mustring but will just as easily sell out their location for a bounty. Even the Navtog has been known to hire a crew for a wetwork job, making every "ally" a potential client, victim, and executioner.

    **A Sea of Rivals.** Navrow's chaotic and predatory nature makes them a threat to any organized power whose shipping they threaten. Their primary antagonist is the Navtog, who actively hunts them to maintain its trade monopoly. However, factions like Colderyn and The Poke will hire mercenaries or other pirates to hunt particularly egregious offenders. Meanwhile, the navies of coastal nations always seek to drive them from their waters.

    **A Viper's Nest.** One of the greatest threats to a Navrow captain is their own crew and fellow captains. The tides of loyalty are fickle and a first mate might murder their captain for a promotion, or a rival crew might sell another's position to the Navtog for a bounty. This internal treachery ensures that every partnership is temporary and every successful voyage paints a target on one's back, making paranoia a basic survival trait.

    #### Adventure Hooks
	___

    **The Trojan Horse.** A Navrow captain hires the party to infiltrate a fortified coastal mission that serves as a refugee sanctuary. Their mission is to pose as refugees themselves, gain entry, and then disable the mission's gate and watchtower from the inside, paving the way for the pirate crew to storm the walls.

    **The Last Witness.** A Navtog Infiltrator wants to hire the party to rescue a Comptroller captured by Navrow pirates, since he has critical information about valuable Navtog trade routes. The party finds the Comptroller willingly participating in the pirates' debauchery, having traded the information for his life and a place among the crew.

    **The Unclaimed Prize.** The party's vessel comes upon two heavily damaged ships locked in a stalemate: a Navrow pirate ship and a Navtog slave ship. Both sides are depleted, and crippled. The Navtog captain offers them salvage rights on the pirate ship, while the Navrow captain offers them a share of the profits from selling the slaves.
