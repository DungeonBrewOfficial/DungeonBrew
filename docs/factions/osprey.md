    *~Naroa Zubizarreta*

The Osprey is a secretive faction of financiers and resource magnates based in Ibunor, quietly manipulating markets and trade policies to suit their interests. Posing as arbiters of consensus and progress, they operate through anonymous operatives and shadowy hierarchies, ensuring no single government or faction can dominate without their consent. Their influence stretches across Dheghom's industrial centers, funding industries, engineering monopolies, and leveraging immense wealth to secure control, all while remaining invisible to the public. While critics accuse them of exploitation and manufacturing dependence, The Osprey frames its actions as necessary for global stability.

Founded by wealthy nobles and magnates responding to the chaos of industrialization, The Osprey rose to power by consolidating wealth and influence under layers of secrecy. Their methods evolved into precise economic manipulation, allowing them to quietly dictate the flow of capital and trade. Today, whispers of their existence fuel distrust from revolutionary factions like Zaharmak and resentment from industrial powers like Colderyn. Yet, their anonymity and reach make them nearly untouchable, securing their role as the unseen architects of Dheghom's economy.

### The Osprey Coalitions

The Osprey is ruled by competing financial coalitions, each with its own vision for controlling global wealth. This section provides the major power blocs within The Roost and how their influence shapes the faction's actions.

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**The Fulcrum.** They see The Osprey as the keystone of civilization, the force that ensures stability in a chaotic world. Their philosophy is one of measured control, shaping economies and governments like a careful hand guiding a blade. They do not want ruin or upheaval; they want balance, and they will break those who threaten it.

**The Mercurials.** For them, uncertainty is the greatest currency. Stability breeds complacency, but collapse? Collapse creates opportunity. They fund wars, orchestrate famines, and drive industries into ruin, not for ideology, but because when the world is burning, there is always profit to be made.

**The Concord.** The only ones who believe in something more than power. They see The Osprey not as manipulators, but as stewards, ensuring that the world does not descend into unchecked exploitation. They act as moderators, deal-brokers, and quiet idealists, but their position is fragile, and many within The Roost see them as naive.

**The Arbitrage.** There is no future, no grand design, only the transaction in front of you. They deal in short-term gain, quick investments, and ruthless efficiency, playing every side as long as the numbers stay in their favor. They do not care who wins, who loses, or what the world becomes; only that their ledger always comes out ahead.

### The Osprey on Other Factions

The Osprey forms calculated alliances to secure control over resources, trade, and influence, avoiding direct conflict. They use investments, lobbying, and subtle manipulation to dominate industrial and political systems, shaping relationships to align with their long-term vision of economic control.

**Celtumpra.** Rumors of Celtumpra's existence are treated as superstition, and their acts of sabotage are often misattributed to other factions. This blindspot prevents The Osprey from recognizing or exploiting Celtumpra effectively.

**Colderyn.** The Osprey funds Colderyn mining and forging intermittently as a means of controlling the global market. Simultaneously, they use predatory loans and market manipulation to keep the cartel dependent and controlled.

**The Ironpenny Poke.** The Factory Barons of The Poke are necessary to ensure a steady flow of manufactured goods. The Osprey, however, considers The Poke's direct, violent approach to labor disputes to be crude and disruptive to the economic stability they prefer to cultivate.

**Mustring.** The Osprey publicly shuns the Mustring syndicate while privately benefiting from the chaos its black-market operations create. The syndicate is a useful force for destabilization that The Osprey would prefer to manipulate more directly.

**Navrow.** Navrow pirates are a disposable tool, sometimes employed by The Osprey to disrupt Navtog shipping and undermine their trade monopolies. However, their inherent lack of loyalty makes them volatile assets, at best.

**The Navtog.** Investing in Navtog's colonial empire is necessary for The Osprey to influence global maritime trade. This alliance is strained by The Navtog's territorial ambitions, which often conflict with The Osprey's preference for more subtle, economic control.

**Zaharmak.** The revolutionary ideology of Zaharmak is a threat to the economic order The Osprey cultivates. Despite this, some Osprey coalitions will arm or fund Zaharmak cells if their acts of terror can be used for short-term profit.

### The Osprey NPCs

The Osprey's members are polished and calculating, moving through the world with quiet precision. Leaders use charm as a weapon, negotiating deals that leave others indebted. Advisors and lawyers manipulate systems and rewrite rules to serve their interests. Enforcers work in the shadows, using blackmail or subtle violence to eliminate threats. Every move is deliberate, every risk measured.

#### Major Players in The Osprey

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**Naroa Zubizarreta.** From a quiet office in The Roost, Naroa Zubizarreta directs The Osprey's financial strategies. An unassuming Ibunese woman, she is rarely seen without an abacus or a ledger, and her composure is not calm, but the utter stillness of someone who views the world as an equation to be solved. She can predict the collapse of a regional economy to the day and calculate the precise number of starving refugees needed to drive down labor costs. To Naroa, atrocities are merely variables in the arithmetic of profit.

**Thaddeus Ezekiel Blythe.** Thaddeus Ezekiel Blythe is the most sought-after Beak in The Osprey, known for brokering deals that leave his partners shackled to the faction for life. His commanding presence and calm, authoritative air are those of a patient predator, and he takes a sincere, personal interest in everyone he negotiates with. Thaddeus discovers a person's most private fears and weaves them into his contracts.

**Marta Chernov.** Marta Chernov genuinely is the unassuming, motherly figure she appears to be; she is also one of The Osprey's longest-serving Eyes. Short, stout, and with a kind face, she offers baked goods and a sympathetic ear at the trade guilds she infiltrates. Having given her entire life to the faction, her loyalty is now one of terrified dependency. The information she extracts, which often leads to the ruin or disappearance of those who confide in her, is simply the price she pays for The Osprey to fund her continued existence.

### The Osprey Roles

Strict order and control are essential to the organization of The Osprey's roles. The leaders are staunch in reigning in lower ranks to match the vision set forth from The Roost while the Feathers and Fledglings jostle for favor in the hopes of rising in station.

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

#### Leaders

**Raptors.** The elite leaders of The Osprey who guide the faction's strategy and decisions through The Roost, their ruling council.

**Wings.** Regional leaders who bridge the gap between Raptors and the faction's operatives, ensuring plans are executed with precision.

#### Operatives

**Beaks.** Diplomats and negotiators who broker alliances, manipulate politics, and secure trade deals.

**Eyes.** Spies and informants placed in rival factions, industries, and governments to gather critical intelligence.

**Talons.** Elite agents handling assassinations, sabotage, and other dangerous operations.

#### Support

**Feathers.** The general membership of The Osprey, composed of financiers, merchants, and minor magnates.

**Fledglings.** New recruits handle minor tasks under close observation, earning their place in the ranks.

### The Osprey Adventures

The Osprey weaves control through subtlety, shaping the world from the shadows. In your campaign, The Osprey works best as an invisible hand, an ally offering tantalizing rewards, or a force whose presence is felt only in the cracks they leave behind. They don't govern openly or enforce power through might; they manipulate markets, control trade, and let wealth speak louder than laws.

Allied with The Osprey, adventurers might find themselves tasked with delicate missions, such as gathering intelligence, undermining rivals, or brokering deals that seem benign until the consequences unfold. The rewards can be immense, but loyalty is assumed, not earned. The Osprey doesn't forgive missteps, and their assistance often comes with obligations the adventurers don't realize they've taken on until it's too late.

As a rival, The Osprey is a faction that rarely moves directly. Their influence might undercut alliances, destabilize local economies, or push factions into conflict, all while their operatives work to stay hidden. Adventurers fighting against The Osprey will often face its pawns (politicians, merchants, or hired agents) without ever seeing the real players. Disrupting their plans isn't just difficult; it often raises the question of whether doing so is worth the fallout.

To make The Osprey resonate, show how far-reaching and intricate their schemes are. A collapsed trade deal might leave a region in economic ruin, or an investment could funnel resources into a faction the adventurers oppose. Whether they're allies, adversaries, or something in between, The Osprey's strength lies in the idea that they're always a step ahead, and the adventurers are only now catching up.

!!! info "Faction Scenario: The Osprey"
    **Primary Operation.** To ensure no single corporate or political entity achieves true dominance. Their goal is to maintain a state of perpetual, manageable conflict among the other major factions. A world of constantly shifting alliances and low-grade warfare creates market volatility, which is the environment where The Osprey profits most through shrewd investments, loans, and financial manipulation.

    #### Methods

	<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

    **Conflict Investment.** The Osprey strategically funds opposing sides of any conflict. They will issue a war loan to an expanding empire while simultaneously financing the smugglers or pirates who prey on that empire's supply lines, ensuring they profit from both the structured war effort and the chaotic disruption it creates.

    **Deniable Intervention.** The Osprey never acts openly. They use layers of secrecy, anonymous operatives, and hired third parties to execute their will, oftentimes disposing of these assets when they are no longer of value. This allows them to sabotage rivals, manipulate political outcomes, and enforce contracts without ever revealing their hand.

    **Debt as a Weapon.** The Osprey acts as the lender of last resort for desperate factions. They issue high-interest loans that are impossible to repay, allowing them to seize corporate assets, control voting shares in guilds, or dictate a nation's trade policy as "collateral."

    #### Complications

    **Unquantifiable Threats.** The Osprey's power relies on predictable, profit-driven motives, a model that fails when confronted with ideological zealots. They cannot reliably control Zaharmak cells, whose acts of terror create chaos that destroys capital rather than creating profitable volatility. Likewise, only vague rumors of Celtumpra's existence makes it difficult to correctly predict or attribute their actions, leaving a blindspot in their risk assessments.

    **War by Other Means.** The competition of Mustring and Navrow controlling a vast shadow economy that lies outside The Osprey's direct influence make it difficult for them to achieve dominance. Meanwhile, The Ironpenny Poke and the Colderyn use brute force to control the markets they operate in, attempting to create closed-loop economies that cut out The Osprey as the middleman.

    **A House Divided.** The Osprey's competing philosophies of how best to achieve success among its internal coalitions create a constant, internal cold war. A single coalition overstepping its bounds could shatter the faction's fragile unity.

    #### Adventure Hooks

    **The Cost of Freedom.** The Osprey has been funding a rebellion in Gane against the Ibunese Empire. To get an arms shipment through the Ibunese blockade in the South Brine, they hire the party to create a maritime incident elsewhere along the coast significant enough to draw away the main fleet. The method of creating this diversion is left entirely to the party.

    **Scorched Earth.** A small merchant guild has defaulted on an Osprey loan, and The Osprey is legally seizing their fleet of ships to sell to another guild. The desperate guild master hires the party to destroy his own ships in port before The Osprey can take possession, choosing to ruin himself and put his sailors out of work rather than allow his assets to fall into his rival's hands.

    **Leverage.** The party acquires a ledger proving a Colderyn official is taking massive bribes from the Osprey. They could sell it to the Ironpenny Poke, who will use it to publicly ruin their rival. They can sell the ledger to his superiors, who will likely kill him. They could attempt to blackmail the corrupt official themselves, trying to profit directly.
