# Mustring

#### [MUS-tring]

<div class="page-split-reversed" markdown="1">

<div class="sidebar-content" markdown="1">

!!! quote ""
    *"The fields grow nothing but dust, and the people owe more than they own or own more than they need. Once, we were their shelter. Now, we are the storm. And if they want to survive, they will learn to weather the storm, or else drown in the deluge."*

    *~Sini Metsäranta*

The Mustring is a sprawling criminal syndicate operating under the guise of rural trade collectives. Behind the illusion of commerce lies an unforgiving system of smuggling, trafficking, and exploitation. Rooted in Tuinleas, they control contraband trade across Oskara, with cells stretching as far as Anterra. Their feudal hierarchy flows from the Ringlord down to the regional Ringmasters, each ruling their territory through violence, coercion, and manipulation. They present themselves as protectors of rural communities. In reality, they thrive on subjugation, enforcing their will through an intricate network of enforcers, spies, and traffickers. Though many factions rely on their services, the Mustring has no true allies. They treat smaller syndicates as tributary vassals and openly clash with industrial powers like The Navtog and The Poke.

Their origins trace back to the Blood Age, when they operated as protectors of the Tonttu in Tuinleas. At the time, they provided safehouses, medicine, and resources to shield rural communities from destruction. Over time, profit overtook protection. Their noble mission curdled into calculated exploitation, turning the vulnerable settlements they once defended into sources of revenue. Now, the Mustring walks a fine line. Internal rivalries threaten to fracture them. External forces seek to dismantle them. Yet their adaptability and ruthlessness ensure they remain a dominant force in Dheghom's underworld.

### Mustring on Other Factions

Mustring forges alliances of convenience, leveraging black-market networks to expand their influence while avoiding entanglements with stronger factions. Fiercely independent, they prioritize profit and dominance over loyalty or long-term cooperation.

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**Celtumpra.** The Mustring view their dealings with Celtumpra agents as business with eccentric and secretive clients who pay well for the transport of strange artifacts. They are largely unaware of the specific nature of their clients or their cargo, and can rarely assess the true value of these goods.

**Colderyn.** The Mustring often smuggle Colderyn goods, both with and without their knowledge, while also acting as a trading partner for anything from slaves to tools. They frequently skim from Colderyn shipments and sell intelligence about their operations to other factions.

**Navrow.** The Mustring trades with Navrow to benefit from their maritime reach. However, they are also direct competitors for control of black markets along the coasts, and Navrow's fractured nature makes them only as trustworthy as whoever is captaining a given ship.

**The Navtog.** The Navtog are a valuable customer who will buy anything from slaves and Navrow ship locations to raw Colderyn materials and goods from The Poke. Unfortunately for Navtog, Mustring also profits from arming and supplying anti-Navtog insurgents, making the relationship a profitable if dangerous balancing act.

**The Osprey.** The Osprey's market manipulation creates economic desperation, which drives new business to the Mustring's black-market services. Mustring views The Osprey as an infestation, often rooting out their spies and informants who attempt to get a hand in Mustring's profits.

**The Ironpenny Poke.** The Poke's constant need for materials makes them a consistent and predictable client. They are also a prime target for theft, and the syndicate benefits doubly by robbing Poke caravans and selling the weapons to the Zaharmak cells that sabotage their factories.

**Zaharmak.** The revolutionaries are a bottomless market for weapons and a convenient source of regional chaos that can be exploited for profit, assuming they have the funds. As such, the syndicate is not above selling out any unreliable Zaharmak cells to the authorities if the bounty is right.

### Mustring NPCs

The Mustring's members rule through coercion, their power disguised as tradition. Ringmasters demand loyalty with a smile, controlling their territories with fear masked as stability. Enforcers and traffickers push the faction's agenda, justifying violence and exploitation as necessary. Laborers and smugglers work at the bottom, too entangled in the system to escape, yet vital to its survival.

#### Major Players in Mustring

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**Aino Mäkinen.** Aino Mäkinen, the Ringlord of Mustring, commands her syndicate not with fear, but with an iron ledger of debts. Her unassuming, drab appearance is that of a municipal clerk, and she has an unsettling habit of remembering every small favor she has ever granted. Raised in the hardship of Tuinleas, she learned that loyalty is fleeting but obligation is permanent. Challenges to her rule are crushed not with assassins, but with a quiet call to one of her thousands of debtors, who are then forced to destroy the rival themselves to settle their own account.

**Gráinne Nic Diarmuid.** As one of Mustring's foremost Reliquarians, the Daoine Sí Gráinne Nic Diarmuid appraises the syndicate's cursed artifacts. Her process is not magical, but empirical; using her test subjects (rivals, debtors, captured spies, betrayers) to determine the precise nature of a curse. Her detailed reports, which note the precise nature of the subject's suffering, are what allow Mustring to set the market price for their most dangerous contraband.

**Thorvald Bjornsson.** The Korathi Dwergar Thorvald Bjornsson is one of the syndicate's many Threshers. His escape from a Colderyn slave camp taught him that any tool can be a weapon, and as a result, he is known for his creative use of industrial equipment in his work. He enforces the syndicate's will with forging tongs, industrial solvents, and high-pressure steam valves, systematically dismantling his victims as a terrifying message to anyone who would cross the Mustring.

### Mustring Roles

Roles in the Mustring have evolved into a sprawling organization that fulfill the particular needs of the faction, such as traffickers and experts in rare goods. While some infighting can occur, the Ringlord tends to keep a tight grip on the reins if they want to be in their position for long, but trust and loyalty are hard to come by.

#### Leaders

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**Ringlord.** The ultimate authority of the Mustring, ruling through fear, wealth, and absolute loyalty.

**Ringmasters.** Territorial leaders who enforce their rule through violence and subjugation while delivering profits to the Ringlord.

**Reeves.** Trusted lieutenants who monitor workers, collect payments, and oversee smuggling, trafficking, and artifact theft, ensuring quotas and enforcing orders.

**Taskmasters.** Enforcers who maintain discipline within the Mustring, punishing betrayal and ensuring loyalty through fear.

#### Operatives

**Fieldhands.** Informants embedded in communities or rival factions, gathering intelligence and expanding the syndicate's influence.

**Shearers.** Thieves who strip communities of their goods and resources.

**Shepherds.** Specialists in human trafficking, managing the capture, transport, and sale of slaves.

**Threshers.** Enforcers responsible for raids, suppressing resistance, and protecting Mustring interests.

**Wayfinders.** Operatives navigating smuggling routes, evading patrols, and mapping safe passages.

#### Support

**Coins.** Financial overseers who manage wealth, contracts, and payouts to maintain the syndicate's resources and loyalty; the coin to the Ringlord is the Coinlord.

**Fencekeepers.** Guards who protect Mustring assets, securing warehouses, hubs, and safehouses from external threats.

**Reliquarians.** Experts in stolen artifacts and rare goods, appraising high-value contraband for eccentric buyers.

**Scribes.** Recordkeepers who map routes, track debts, and ensure operational secrecy; the scribe to the Ringlord is the Scribelord.

### Mustring Adventures

Mustring is a faction built on desperation and control, thriving where rural decay meets criminal ambition. When you bring Mustring into your campaign, think about how their influence grows quietly through rural communities, black-market trade, and coercive power structures. Their power comes from knowing how to turn desperation into dependency, offering protection with one hand while holding the whip with the other and ensuring that no one under their protection has the means to resist or escape.

If adventurers ally with Mustring, it's never about trust. A Ringmaster might offer them lucrative work, such as eliminating rivals, securing smuggling routes, or mediating disputes, but their generosity is transactional. Rewards come at a cost, often dragging the party deeper into a web of debts and obligations they can't escape.

As a force in the world, Mustring exerts quiet but unrelenting pressure. They aren't a storm; they're erosion: slowly reshaping the communities they touch. Villages reliant on their protection might turn to the adventurers for help but fear the consequences of disloyalty. Smugglers operating on Mustring's orders could pull the party into territorial disputes or draw the ire of other factions.

Highlight Mustring's feudal hierarchy and the tension between their roles as protectors and oppressors. A Ringmaster enforcing loyalty might feel justified, even noble, while their enforcers treat rebellion as a personal affront. Mustring works best when adventurers see the cost of defiance and the reward of compliance.

!!! info "Faction Scenario: Mustring"
    **Primary Operation.** To be the indispensable, clandestine logistical network for every major power on both continents. Their goal is not to rule, but to be the shadow economy that profits from every war, every famine, and every illicit desire, ensuring all sides become dependent on their services.

    #### Methods
	
	<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

    **Logistical Blackmail.** The Mustring maintain a vast network of smugglers, hidden routes, and corrupt officials, allowing them to move anything, anywhere, for a price. They leverage the operational secrets of their clients to manipulate prices, extort favors, and ensure their own indispensability.

    **Conflict Profiteering.** The syndicate's core business model is to fuel both sides of any conflict. They sell weapons to insurgents while selling slaves captured by those same insurgents back to their oppressors. They trade in food, medicine, intelligence, and contraband, profiting from every transaction regardless of the moral implications.

    **Neutrality as a Weapon.** The Mustring maintain a strict public neutrality, with their only allegiance being to profit. This allows them to operate in territories where other factions cannot, acting as intermediaries and information brokers. They leverage this position to play their clients against each other.

    #### Complications

    **Precarious Partnerships.** The Mustring's business model requires them to work with volatile and untrustworthy partners. Zaharmak cells attempt to co-opt their smuggling crews for revolution. Navrow pirates, once clients for smuggled goods, now frequently seize Mustring shipments for themselves. Even their largest clients, like the Navtog and Colderyn, will not hesitate to eliminate a caravan and its crew to cover their tracks or avoid payment.

    **The Gilded Cage.** The "legitimate" corporate powers see the Mustring's black market as a threat to their control. The Osprey uses its financial power to fund mercenary companies to disrupt key Mustring routes. Simultaneously, industrial guilds like The Ironpenny Poke and the Colderyn constantly try to reverse-engineer the Mustring's methods and establish their own covert logistical networks, aiming to cut out the middlemen.

    **Organizational Rot.** The Mustring's strict neutrality and focus on profit have created a culture of betrayal. High-ranking syndicate members frequently sell out their own colleagues to rivals like The Osprey for a higher payday. Lower-level crews, seeing no loyalty from the top, often skim from shipments, sell cargo on the side, or abandon dangerous runs, creating constant internal security risks and operational failures.

    #### Adventure Hooks

    **Rented Mule.** The Mustring hire the party for a simple smuggling run: transport a cargo of "medical supplies" to a city suffering from famine and disease. However, the crates contain not medicine, but a highly addictive narcotic the syndicate intends to use to create a new, permanent market for their product.

    **A Little Problem.** The party discovers that a local orphanage is a front for a Mustring child trafficking ring, selling children into industrial servitude. The local authorities are paid off and will not act. The party is the only force capable of intervening, but a direct attack on a Mustring operation will bring the full weight of the ruthless syndicate down upon them.

    **For the Right Price.** A high-ranking Mustring logistics officer has been captured by the Navtog. This officer holds the secrets to dozens of operations, including the supply lines for several "heroic" rebel groups the party may sympathize with. A Zaharmak cell wants to hire the party to infiltrate the Navtog prison and assassinate the officer to protect the revolutionary network. The Mustring also contact the party, offering a reward to rescue him, preserving their profitable enterprise.
