## How This Works

The form is organized into three sections: Guild Overview, Membership, and Leadership Structure.

### Guild Overview

**Guild Name** - Self-explanatory.

**Guild Type** - Six specializations:

- **Assassins' Guild** - Specializes in assassination jobs. Could be highest-bidder, covert government operation, or religious in nature.
- **Black Market** - Trades embargoed or high-taxed goods, cursed items, slaves, and other illegal merchandise.
- **Explorers' Society** - Buys, sells, or guides others to unique locations like ancient ruins, lost dungeons, and hidden temples.
- **Spy Ring** - Specializes in infiltration, forgery, cryptography, and counterintelligence. May be highest-bidder or government sanctioned.
- **Thieves' Guild** - Jack-of-all-trades catch-all for other guild types or specializations.
- **Trading Company** - Uses thieves for counter-theft measures or as a front for other guild types.

**Guild Alignment** - Identifies if your guild is largely good, neutral, or evil. Can include law or chaos if desired.

**Primary Guild Activities** - Notes field. What major quests/missions/jobs are being undertaken? What types of jobs is the guild willing or unwilling to take?

### Membership

**Responsibilities** - Member obligations. Examples include:

- **Temporary Dues** - New members or those in trouble may give more earnings to the guild.
- **Obey Leadership** - Almost always yes, but chaotic guilds with poor leadership may differ.
- **Participate in Guild Activities** - Some guilds allow more freelance work than others.

**Entitlements** - What the guild provides to recruit and retain members. Some may be role-based (Master members get private rooms). Examples:

- **Protection** - Members expect backup if they're in trouble.
- **Loans** - Members can borrow from the guild, usually at discounted rates.
- **Legal Help** - Does the guild bail out or break out caught members?

**Consequences** - Applied when members fail responsibilities. Consider consequences that don't interrupt ability to work or bring profit to the guild.

**Thieves' Cant** - Special words, phrases, or signs that distinguish your guild from others.

### Leadership Structure

**Structure Type** - Six organizational models:

- **Rigid Hierarchy** - Guild Master as central authority.
- **Devolved Hierarchy** - Multiple hierarchies within the same overarching guild.
- **Hierarchical Conglomerate** - Multiple groups (each under a master) unite under one roof to combine skills.
- **Core Criminal Group** - Masters share leadership with no definitive leader.
- **Organized Criminal Network** - More of a gathering of like-minded people than a formal structure.

**Structure Model** - Three approaches:

- **Hierarchical** - Typical guild structure with graded ranks.
- **Cultural** - Insular, requiring members from same neighborhood, city, species, etc.
- **Enterprise** - Organized like a legitimate business to ensure profitability.

### Guild Ranks

With expected levels and responsibilities:

- **Guild Master (10-20)** - Oversees the guild, makes final decisions.
- **Under Master (8-16)** - Second in command. May oversee satellite guilds. Position may be unfilled or split among multiple Under Masters.
- **Lieutenant (7-15)** - Oversees masters. Reports to Under Master or Guild Master. Larger guilds may have multiple.
- **Master Assassin (7-15)** - Identifies and approves assassination contracts. Manages assassination jobs.
- **Master Enforcer (7-15)** - Maintains discipline. Handles extortion, robbery, and kidnapping jobs.
- **Master Fencer (7-15)** - Tracks supply and demand for profitability. Manages smuggling jobs.
- **Master of Thralls (7-15)** - Acquires, maintains, and sells slaves. Manages slavery operations.
- **Master of Veils (7-15)** - Runs legal or semi-legal fronts. Manages gambling, expeditions, fraud, and front operations.
- **Master Purser (7-15)** - Tracks treasury. Works with Master Fencer and Guild Master. Manages loansharking.
- **Master Thief (7-15)** - Locates valuable or vulnerable targets. Manages burglary, larceny, and pickpocketing jobs.
- **Master Warder (7-15)** - Handles guild security, passwords, traps. Manages protection and retaliation jobs.
- **Spymaster (7-15)** - Acquires and reports information. May sell it or use it for guild benefit. Manages impersonation, blackmail, and forgery jobs.
- **Taskmaster (7-15)** - Keeps members active, ensures jobs are completed, oversees daily operations. Acts as handler for members. Manages heists and racketeering. Larger guilds may have multiple.
- **Conductor (Wetnurse) (5-10)** - Oversees new recruits, handles training and discipline until they become full members.
- **Headhunter (5-10)** - Locates new recruits. Works with Conductor and Masters to find needed talent.
- **Member (3-7)** - Rank-and-file workers who follow orders and meet quotas.
- **Thug (Muscle) (3-12)** - Handles physical tasks: guard duty, intimidation. May not have rogue levels.
- **Recruits (1-3)** - New talent showing promise, skill, or connections within the guild.
- **Allies and Contacts (Any level)** - Either paid for contract work or involuntarily involved through extortion.
