### **History**

Cyclopian myth describes their Bloom Age origins within the southern lands of Oskara, asserting the Ori Balarika mountains as their ancestral homeland, granted by their gods and held since time immemorial. Early Cyclopian communities mastered their varied lands, from mountains to coasts, developing societies centered on strength and perhaps early forms of statecraft. Their histories claim the Ori Balarika range as the heart of their territory, a belief central to their identity.

The Blood Age brought widespread chaos, forcing conflict upon the Cyclopian ancestors. Records from this era are fragmented, but Baleran tradition holds that defense against encroaching peoples, possibly the proto-Avenarians or others, necessitated an alliance with the Dwergar peoples situated to the north. Their strength and commitment in fulfilling their side of the pact, expecting a similar honor from the Dwergar. Although victory was achieved, the alliance fractured almost immediately. The Dwergar occupied the majority of the Ori Balarika range, territory the Cyclopians considered theirs by right. This betrayal unified the Cyclopian communities into a single nation, driven by the shared grievance over the lost mountains and the perceived treachery of the Dwergar.

The Blight Age has been shaped entirely by the Ho Polemos against Korath. Centuries have been defined by relentless, costly campaigns aimed at retaking the Ori Balarika. Major efforts, including the First and Second Sieges against Kizar Korath, are remembered as attempts to liberate their lands from the Dwergar occupiers, who relied on formidable defenses. Baleros adapted readily to the era's new technologies; black powder and advanced siege engines were adopted and refined specifically as tools to overcome Korath's mountain fortifications. This long, singular focus forged Baleros into a highly militarized state, its resources consistently directed towards the northern conflict.

Now, millennia after the original betrayal, reports suggest the Dwergar kingdom of Korath is weakened by internal division and plague. For Baleros, this presents a potential turning point in their long struggle. The ancient grievance remains the core of their foreign policy and national identity. However, the political landscape is dangerous, making preparations for renewed efforts to reclaim the Ori Balarika unwieldy, despite the perceived opportunity to finally succeed where generations past have failed.

### **Government**

Baleros is a Stratocracy, ruled through the Strategoi Council, which is composed of the highest-ranking military leader from each major Cyclopian Polis or Khora. This council directs national strategy, allocates resources, and oversees Ho Polemos against Korath. While nominally a collective, representation is influenced by the contributing power of each member's domain, with the Strategos of Ostiros often holding significant sway due to the capital's importance. The Council's focus remains fixed on the conflict that forged their nation.

From amongst its members, the Strategoi Council elects a supreme commander, often titled the Polemarchos or War-King, to lead the nation's military efforts. This position carries a fixed term of twelve years, and an individual cannot be immediately re-elected, ensuring circulation of command. Constitutionally, the War-King role is only activated during times of war; however, as Baleros considers Ho Polemos to have been continuous since the Great Betrayal in the Blood Age, the position has been perpetually filled for millennia.

The conflict with Korath has been institutionalized as the central pillar of Baleran statecraft. While the Polemarchos provides unified military command, real political power resides within the Council, leading to constant maneuvering among the Strategoi for influence and position ahead of each election cycle. This system prioritizes experienced military leadership above all other aspects of Cyclopian life.

### **Interesting Facts**

- Epic poetry is often performed in public venues, sometimes with drums and accompanying music. These poems may recount myths of the Bloom Age and Blood Age about the gods or heroic cycles and battle. For many Cyclopeans, the only path to immortality is to become part of these cycles.

- Precise geometric patterns hold significant aesthetic value in Baleran crafts, from textiles to pottery to architectural stonework. Some Baleran philosophers argue this preference stems from the Cyclopian single eye finding measurable order and symmetry particularly visually harmonious or easier to perceive than more complex, asymmetrical designs.

- Public debate is a high art among the cyclopes, considered a vital skill for leaders and citizens alike. In the nation's cities and larger settlements, open forums are common spaces where vigorous philosophical contests are held and the great epic poetry cycles are performed, often drawing large, critical audiences.

### **Current Challenges**

- **The Ho Polemos.** Recent intelligence suggests that Korath is significantly weakened. This news has created pressure within the Strategoi Council. Hawkish factions demand an immediate, full-scale assault to finally reclaim the Ori Balarika, arguing such an opportunity may never come again. More cautious members, however, warn that Dwergar defenses remain formidable and that another failed siege could shatter Baleros for generations.

- **The Merchant Question.** The endless war is funded more and more by the wealth of a growing merchant class based in Ostiros. Enriched by trade in quarried stone, logistical contracts, and the production of black powder weaponry based on foreign designs, these powerful families have begun to leverage their economic power to demand political influence. This has created an internal struggle between the traditional military aristocracy of the Strategoi Council and the new merchant lords, a conflict of influence fought with coin rather than steel.

- **The Wild Tribes.** The Council's authority is tenuous in the western plains and southern wilderness. The tribes in these territories are fiercely independent, resisting centralized rule and often evading tax collectors. This cultural divide has deepened with the state's adoption of black powder, as many tribes hold to older warrior traditions and view firearms as a cowardly departure from the ideal of individual strength. The state needs the resources and hardy warriors these tribes produce, but its attempts to impose control or enforce new military doctrines are met with defiance.

### **Baleran Characters**

As you create a Baleran character or NPC, consider the following:

**The Endless War.** Baleros was forged by the Ho Polemos and exists solely to see it concluded. Almost every aspect of Cyclopian society is shaped by the ancient grievance against Korath and the singular goal of retaking the Ori Balarika mountains. Are you supporting the war effort as a soldier, an engineer, a strategist, or a provisioner? Do you support the war effort, or has the endless conflict made you weary or pragmatic? What is your view of the Dwergar who occupy your ancestral lands?

**Military Technocracy.** Expertise in the tools of modern warfare is ingrained in Baleros's military strategy. While traditional strength is respected, victory may come from controlling the design, production, or deployment of new technology. The military state relies on its engineers and artillerists as much as its commanders. What is your relationship with this technology? Do you believe technology enhances Cyclopian strength or risks supplanting it? How do you reconcile the destruction caused by modern war technology?

**Faith and Duty.** For many, Baleran faith is inseparable from the Ho Polemos. Although frequent worship and rites center on the imprisoned god, Orophylax has not been seen in millennia. Do you view freeing Orophylax as a duty? Do you worship another Baleran deity, such as Helionax or Petrichos, or do you worship gods at all?

### **Religious Practices**

Cyclopian faith in Baleros is inseparable from the Ho Polemos. Worship centers on Orophylax, whose sanctum within the Ori Balarika mountains was stolen by the Dwergar during the Blood Age. Baleran tradition holds that Orophylax is entombed within the mountains, its divine suffering now manifesting as volcanic activity, proof, to the Balerans, of its torment under Korathi occupation. State rituals emphasize remembrance of this Great Betrayal, veneration of the imprisoned god, and the divine call to liberate the lost peaks.

While the nation is united in reverence for Orophylax and the cause of reclamation, other deities provide more immediate divine connection. Helionax, the lion of the Chrysochorion Grasses, is invoked by warriors. Petrichos, the toad within the Ombrochora forests, is tied to rituals for rain and renewal.

### **Cities and Sites of Interest**

The lands of Baleros stretch from the high peaks of the Ori Balarika mountains, across vast inland plains, and deep into the southern wilderness that meets the eastern sea. The nation's sites include its large port capital, vast interior stone quarries, nomadic settlements on the plains, and the fortified passes guarding its northern border. This geography has created a state divided between its developed eastern lands, southern and western wilderness, and the heavily garrisoned lands of the north.

#### **_Ostiros_**

The capital of Baleros is a large port city on the nation's eastern coast. It is known for its grand, orderly avenues and geometrically precise stone architecture, built on a scale suited to its towering Cyclopian inhabitants. As the seat of the Strategoi Council, the city's political life is defined by a persistent rivalry between the established military families and a wealthy merchant class. Its markets are known as a meeting point for soldiers on leave, herdsmen from the western plains, and agents of foreign factions.

#### **_Pylai Penthous_**

The primary staging ground for the war against Korath and the most heavily fortified pass on the northern border. Its approaches are a graveyard of military hardware, littered with the wreckage of siege engines from generations of failed campaigns. The fortifications are carved directly into the mountain rock, vast complexes that house the permanent garrison of the Akrítai, those sworn to a lifetime of service on the frontier. It is from here that every major assault on the Ori Balarika is launched.

#### **_Chrysochorion Plains_**

The vast, semi-arid grasslands of southern Baleros, prone to long seasons of drought followed by torrential rains. The plains are home to semi-nomadic Cyclopian tribes who follow immense herds of migratory beasts across the landscape. The culture of these tribes is shaped by constant movement and the need for self-sufficiency, valuing fierce independence and a deep knowledge of the land. The Strategoi Council struggles to govern these tribes directly, content to demand tribute and recruit their hardiest warriors when they can be found.

### **The Strategoi Council**

Baleran politics is dominated by the ambitions of its ancient political parties, formal alliances of Strategoi who constantly maneuver for control of the Council. The primary goal of each party is to elect one of its own as Polemarchos, giving them control over the nation's armies and resources. The endless rivalries between these parties have created a state of political paralysis, resulting in intrigue, shifting alliances, and outright sabotage. This long-standing stalemate has recently been shattered by the emergence of new, powerful players, creating a chaotic and unpredictable five-sided political war.

#### **_The Patrikoi_**

The oldest political party in Baleros, the Patrikoi's platform is built on the legacy of the "heroic lines." They are the arch-traditionalists, arguing that only those with the blood of heroes possess the legitimacy to rule. They are masters of the Council's ancient procedures, but their rigid adherence to tradition has left them struggling to adapt to the new political realities, forcing them into a desperate battle to maintain their fading influence.

#### **_The Akropolitai_**

The ancient rivals of the Patrikoi, the Akropolitai party was formed by meritocratic commanders from the northern garrisons. Their platform is one of ruthless pragmatism and proven results over ancestry. For centuries, they were the primary opposition, but the rise of new factions has threatened their position, forcing them to consider uneasy alliances and compromises that betray their meritocratic ideals in the simple pursuit of power.

#### **_Antigonus's Legion_**

A new and disruptive political force, this faction is not a true party but a personality cult built around the charismatic and ferociously successful general, Antigonus. Sick of the political stalemate, Antigonus has used his recent victories and the fanatical loyalty of his veteran legions to bypass the Council entirely, wielding his army as a political party in its own right. His meteoric rise has terrified the old parties and thrown the entire political landscape into chaos.

#### **_The Synoikía_**

Formed in direct response to the threat posed by Antigonus, the Synoikía is a desperate and uneasy alliance of several lesser "heroic lines" who were not powerful enough to lead the Patrikoi but now fear their own extinction. They are conservatives scrambling for survival, banding together to create a voting bloc strong enough to balance the scales. Their loyalties are fractured, and their alliance is a marriage of convenience that could collapse at any moment.

#### **_The Chorēgoí_**

The "Sponsors" are the external force that profits from the chaos. This class of wealthy industrialists and merchants holds no seats on the Council but funds all sides of the political conflict. With the rise of new factions, their role has become more important than ever. They throw their immense wealth behind whichever player or party seems most likely to grant them the lucrative contracts and economic policies they desire, ensuring that no matter who wins the political war, they control the economy.
