### **History**

During the Bloom Age, the Tonttu existed as a collection of independent agrarian communes on the eastern coast of Oskara. Their society has always been one of agriculture and pastoral life, focused on their local deities, while their culture developed in relative isolation.

The Blood Age brought an end to this isolation. The Ogre civil wars spurred the Lutin schism, which marked the beginning of retaliatory Ogre raids against the Tonttu who remained. The Tonttu were then subjugated by the expanding Ekarajya Empire, beginning a long history of imperial vassalage. After the empire's collapse, the Tonttu lands were contested by warring petty kingdoms as Ogre raids intensified. In this era of anarchy, the Mustring formed as a shadow government to protect Tonttu communities. This period ended with the Marcian Conquest, which consolidated the territory before the newly-founded Kingdom of Bethune defeated the Marcians and inherited Tuinleas as a spoil of war.

In the Blight Age, Bethune formalized its control over the territory, and the relationship shifted to one of direct colonial management. As Bethune's administration became entrenched, the Mustring's original protective purpose became corrupted. The organization devolved into a predatory criminal syndicate, exploiting the same communities it was created to defend and becoming another oppressor of its own people.

Today, Tuinleas exists in a state of institutionalized dependency. Though the Ogre threat has largely been ended by the Ibunese Empire's conquering of Gane, Bethune maintains its control, justifying its presence through tradition while exploiting Tuinleas as an agricultural breadbasket to feed its industrial heartlands. Decades of this rule have fostered a deep-seated "learned helplessness" in the Tonttu, whose identity has been shaped by a history of being perpetually conquered and controlled.

___

### **Government**

The governance of Tuinleas is composed of an official Bethunian colonial administration and a de facto native government operating as a shadow state. The formal government is administered by Bethune and led by a Viceroy, a political appointee who represents the authority of the Divine Council. The Viceroy's decrees are enforced by a Lord Protector, who serves as the military commander of the Bethunian garrison stationed in the nation.

The unofficial Tonttu government is the Mustring, a syndicate led by a figure known as the Ringlord. The Mustring functions as a parallel authority, with its own hierarchical structure and leadership. While the Bethunian administration is the recognized legal power, the Mustring operates as the governing body for the Tonttu populace.

### **Interesting Facts**

- Many older Tonttu structures, such as well-houses and community hearths, incorporate stones scavenged from the ruins of Ekarajyan and Marcian-era fortifications. It is a common folk practice to place these foreign stones with their carved imperial insignias facing inward or downward, hidden from sight.

- The Tonttu have a secret, informal language used to convey messages publicly without the notice of their Bethunian rulers. It is not a spoken language, but a system of contextual symbols using natural objects from the land itself, such as a knotted daisy or a straw tied around an alfalfa stalk. While this language is known to all Tonttu, including many of the Mustring, its hyper-local variations make it a useful tool for quiet communication.

- The Tonttu have no grand historical epics about great kings or victorious battles. Their most enduring folk hero is Vekku-Pekka (Trickster Pekka), who is not a warrior, but a clever trickster who uses his wits to outsmart Bethunian tax collectors and Mustring thugs, which often centers around making these larger or more vicious aggressors appear foolish.

### **Current Challenges**

- **A Crisis of Governance.** The Tonttu people are caught between their indifferent colonial administrators and their own predatory shadow government. The Bethunian Viceroyalty is a revolving door of short-term appointments, exploiting Tuinleas for its resources while the Mustring preys upon what little wealth they have. Meanwhile, like the Viceroy, the position of Ringlord changes hands frequently, and with it, the syndicate's methods oscillate between protection and predation, leaving the Tonttu with no reliable authority or stability.

- **Agricultural Decline.** Centuries of Bethunian-mandated intensive farming have led to widespread soil degradation across Tuinleas. The demand for agricultural tithes has stripped the land of its nutrients, resulting in steadily decreasing crop yields and the beginning of ecological collapse in the nation's most fertile regions. This slow-moving disaster presents a long-term threat of famine and the failure of the Tonttu's primary resource.

- **The Ibunese Question.** The recent Ibunese conquest of Gane has created a new threat across the strait. This threat has ignited a fierce debate within the Bethunian Divine Council over the future of the occupation. One faction argues for an increased military presence to counter the threat of Ibunese espionage and sabotage, while another argues for a full withdrawal to cut the costs of a garrison that no longer fights Ogres.

### **Tonttu Characters**

As you create a Tonttu character or NPC, consider the following:

**A Life Between Tyrants.** Native Tonttu are confronted with the daily reality of being expected to serve two masters. How do you navigate the demands of your Bethunian overlords and the Mustring shadow government? Do you pay homage to one, both, or neither? Have you found yourself at odds with these authorities? How do their expectations affect your daily life?

**The Scars of Conquest.** Millennia of subjugation have left their mark on the Tonttu soul. How has this history of being conquered shaped you and your family? Do you accept the lot of your people or struggle against it? Do you feel confined or comforted by the cultural narrative of subjugation?

**The Fading Ways.** The Tonttu identity is rooted in its folkloric traditions, but many feel they are fading under the weight of colonial rule. Do you still feel connected to your people's culture? Do you seek the counsel of a local Opastaja, tell the stories of Vekku-Pekka, or leave offerings for your gods? Do you feel connected to the land? Do you seek to understand the modern world?

### **Religious Practices**

Tonttu faith is a patchwork of idiosyncratic, folkloric traditions, rites, and superstitions maintained by the Opastaja. Religious practices are conducted at a village or family level, centered on the local deities whose sanctums dot the landscape. Their worship is not internalized as transactional, however, it is driven by a deep-seated belief that these ancient powers must be acknowledged and appeased, lest the delicate balance of the world be upset.

The Tonttu recognize greater powers like Veden Emo and Tuulen Kulkija, who they perceive as indifferent to their struggles, while seeing the Ember Man as a reflection of the vulnerability of the gods. The Tonttu generally perform rites out of reverence without hope of reward, but maintain a superstitious fear of what might happen should the old ways be ignored. Their faith is a projection of their own anxieties; they see a good harvest as a sign that their rituals have successfully maintained the world's order for another season.

### **Cities and Sites of Interest**

Tuinleas is a nation of rolling, fertile plains, temperate coastal lands, and scattered woodlands. The landscape is dotted with small Tonttu agricultural villages that have stood for centuries, overshadowed by the architectural remnants of the empires that have conquered them.

#### **_Tutbuton_**

The capital of Tuinleas's history of conquest is rendered in stone. Its architecture is a chaotic mishmash of the successive empires that have ruled it, from the ancient foundations of the Ekarajya to the grandiose fortifications of the Marcians and now layered with the functional administrative buildings of the current Bethunian occupation. The city is the seat of the Bethunian Viceroy and the powerless Council of Reeves, a place of deference and resentment where Tonttu locals navigate the predatory politics of both their colonial masters and the Mustring syndicate.

#### **_Tammenlehto_**

Once a thriving Tonttu village whose life revolved around its sacred oak tree, Tammenlehto is now a field of smoldering ruins. The village and its guardian spirit, Olkiukko, were destroyed during the War of the Hundred Kings, but the embers still burn. This act of sacrilege corrupted the deity into the Tatarigami known as the Ember Man. The ruins are now a cursed and feared place, haunted by a spirit trapped in a compulsive loop of its own fiery immolation.

#### **_Ludgor Castle_**

On the Bethunian border deep within the Kylmakorpi Wood, stand the ruins of Ludgor Castle. The ruin is remnant of the millennia-old Ekarajyan fortress, Lohita Durgam. Its architectural history shows signs of reconstruction from the War of the Hundred Kings and the Marcian Conquest, yet the local redstone used still hails back to its original name. While the ruined fortress appears to be abandoned, it serves as the stronghold of the Mustring. Beneath the crumbling keep lies a fortified bunker from which the Ringlord directs the syndicate's criminal operations and Tuinleas's shadow government.

### **Factions of Tuinleas**

Power in Tuinleas is a constant grinding conflict between the official colonial, if indifferent, government, a native, predatory shadow state, and the informal, cultural authorities who are powerless to elicit change.

#### **_The Bethunian Administration_**

The official authority in Tuinleas is the colonial administration appointed by the Divine Council of Bethune. This body is composed of two primary figures: the Viceroy, who serves as the bureaucratic head of the government, and the Lord Protector, the military commander of the Bethunian garrison. The Viceroyalty is often a temporary political stepping stone for Bethunian nobles, resulting in a high turnover rate and a general apathy toward the Tonttu people. The administration's primary function is to ensure the efficient collection of agricultural tithes and the suppression of any native industry that could challenge Bethunian authority.

#### **_The Mustring_**

The Mustring is the de facto shadow government of Tuinleas and the nation's most powerful native faction. While Bethune sees them as a simple criminal syndicate, the Mustring operates as a parallel authority, with a volatile Ringlord at its head. They collect their own taxes in the form of protection money, enforce their own laws through violence, and manipulate the Council of Reeves from the shadows. The nature of their rule is unpredictable, shifting with the personality of each new Ringlord, but their existence is a constant, unavoidable reality for every Tonttu.

#### **_Council of Reeves_**

The Council of Reeves is the local Tonttu governance tolerated by the Bethunian administration. Composed of mayors and governors from the various Tonttu settlements, the Council is ostensibly responsible for managing local affairs and conveying the needs of the populace to the Viceroy. In reality, it is a mostly powerless body, its members often chosen for their compliance with the Viceroy rather than their leadership. The Council is caught between the demands of the Viceroy and the threats of the Mustring, forced to serve two masters and fully trusted by neither.

#### **_The Opastaja_**

The Opastaja are the local healers, leaders, religious officials, and arbiters of day-to-day disputes, all while keeping the ways of Tonttu culture alive. This faction has no formal structure and their power is neither military nor political, but cultural. They work, ostensibly, to guide their communities, but have served for millennia to preserve the Tonttu's oral history, folkloric traditions, and religious rites. They are the guardians of Tonttu identity, and their quiet, stubborn refusal to give up that identity is the closest thing to resistance or challenge that the Tonttu people can hope for.
