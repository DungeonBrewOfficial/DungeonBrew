### **History**

During the Bloom Age, humans in Bethune's lands lived in scattered tribes, sustained by agriculture and hunting. Guided by deities tied to rivers, forests, and hills, these small, isolated communities had no recorded conflicts or kingdoms.

The Blood Age saw the rise of small feudal kingdoms throughout what would become modern Bethune. Over the course of this Age, these lands would be conquered and unified under the Ekarajyan Empire. The empire's Great Collapse (c. 1,900 BdA) plunged the territory into the chaos of the War of the Hundred Kings. This power vacuum was exploited by Marcia, which conquered the old imperial capital of Bakarsha and established it as their own colonial seat of power, renaming it Bellegrad. The First Kingdom of Bethune was founded in 2,580 BdA when the native Oskaran noble, Alaric Percival Ashbourne, led a successful rebellion, drove the Marcian occupiers from his homeland and captured Bellegrad as his capital. For the next four centuries, the Ashbourne Dynasty waged a constant war of consolidation against the other successor states of the fallen empire.

For Bethunians, the Blight Age began with the transformation of the start of the Great Plague or, more often, the death of the Ashbourne dynasty. The God King rose to power and restructured the government into a theocracy. Subsequent plagues reinforced his rule, while the adoption of black powder revolutionized Bethune's military, enabling the final conquest of the remaining independent human states in the region.

Bethune's industrial progress has been tempered by recurring plagues like the Lingering and Iron Lung and, currently, the Withering. Ongoing tensions with Askara, Ibunor, and Steinlund divide the attention of the Divine Council, limiting the reach of the powerful nation as it grapples with mounting and disparate conflicts. While the Divine Council remains ostensibly loyal to the God King, the coming of yet another plague threatens the stability of his rule.

___

### **Government**

Bethune is a theocratic monarchy led by the God King, a divine figure who remains reclusive within his palace, the Kyrie. While his decrees are considered divine law, the day-to-day governance of the nation is handled by the Divine Council, an oligarchy of thirteen nobles from Bethune's most prominent families.

The Council's authority is derived from their ancient noble titles and vast land holdings, which in the modern era include the nation's factories, mines, and black powder armories. The Council oversees all state functions, appointing regional administrators, magistrates, and the Peace Officers who handle everyday law enforcement.

Bethune's enforcement is visibly divided. The Plague Wardens serve as the Divine Council's state police, responsible for enforcing laws, suppressing industrial unrest, and managing quarantines during plague outbreaks. Separate from the Council's authority is the Immortal Guard, an elite, secretive order whose sole function is to guard the Kyrie and the person of the God King.

### **Interesting Facts**

- Originally formed during the Second Death to enforce quarantines, the Plague Wardens have since evolved into the Divine Council's state police. Their duties now include any task The Divine Council sets them to, which often involves containing or controlling the public, particularly during times of unrest. Ultimately, the Kidham family controls this body, making them a staunch and immoveable structure within the Divine Council.

- The God King's protectors are officially known as the Kyrie Guard, a body formed by the Divine Council in the early days of their divine ruler's ascension when the public tried to sneak into the Kyrie to see their god. Now, they are known colloquially as the Immortal Guard, as those who are selected are thought to live forever in The God King's service. Although seen as a great honor, the perceived loss of anyone recruited to this prestigious role has resulted in a custom of a symbolic funeral for sons and daughters who will never be seen again.

- Plagues in Bethune are not a single, apocalyptic event; they are a chronic, recurring fact of life, like a change in the seasons. Every major industrial city has permanent, consecrated Plague Pits maintained by the Wardens, and public etiquette is dominated by plague-conscious rituals. It is common to see merchants conducting business through 'plague windows' or citizens striking their own chests as a formal greeting to 'prove' the health of their lungs.

### **Current Challenges**

- **Askaran War.** The northern border with Askara is a lawless marchland in which Bethunian nobles prosecute private wars of land acquisition against the Askaran Khandas, while Askaran raiding parties use guerrilla tactics to strike at supply lines and isolated settlements. The conflict is a constant reality of ambushes, burned villages, and skirmishes over old Ekarajyan watchtowers, a low-intensity struggle that Bethune's conventional military struggles to contain.

- **The Withering.** This latest plague spreads rapidly through Bellegrad's slums, overwhelming the Plague Wardens. Its primary symptom is a debilitating physical weakness that progresses slowly but inevitably toward death. The Divine Council has scrambled to contain the outbreak, imposing quarantines on the poor districts while ensuring the Noble Districts remain protected, deepening the city's already stark class divisions.

- **Steinlund Conflict.** Since Bethune attempted to build a railway through Zwerge territory in 671 BTA, the eastern border has been defined by Zwerge resistance. This takes the form of sabotage against rail lines and ironworks near Loxmere, guerrilla attacks launched from mountain tunnels against Bethunian patrols, and diplomatic obstruction via Kazak. Bethune maintains a heavy military presence, but the conflict remains a costly stalemate.

- **Ibunese Cold War.** Following the inconclusive Ten-Years War (535-545 BTA), Bethune and Ibunor remain locked in a cold war defined by an industrial arms race. Both nations pour vast resources into developing black powder weaponry and fortifying their shared border with modern defenses. Industrial espionage is rampant, with spies from both sides attempting to steal technological secrets, making border cities like the Ashbourne Armory centers of paranoia and counter-intelligence operations.

### **Bethunian Characters**

As you create a Bethunian character or NPC, consider the following:

**Social Standing.** Social standing in Bethune often limits how high you can climb. Aristocracy varies from high to low, from members of the Divine Council, to lowly lords of rural communities whose lands were conquered centuries ago. The wealthier classes, merchants and guildsmen, have some small say in their lives, but it is the common poor, the factory workers, farmers, and laborers who struggle most with little to show except their continued survival.

**The Sliding Scale of Faith.** The God King is the state-approved deity of Bethune. Are you a fervent believer who views his divine authority as infallible, or do you question his role during crises like the Withering? Perhaps your faith is rooted in the traditions of your outlying community in a remote portion of Bethune?

**Conflict and War.** Soldiering is in the blood of Bethune, with conflicts on multiple fronts. Are you a soldier defending the northern border from Askaran raids? Are you stationed in Tuinleas, protecting one of Bethune's most valuable assets? Or do you merely support the war effort as a railway worker trying to lay track in Steinlund or as a merchant delivering supplies to an outlying fort on the Ibunor border?

### **Religious Practices**

Bethunian religious practice is a sliding scale of faith dictated by geography, class, and necessity.

In the capital of Bellegrad, faith is a structured, political affair. The Divine Council maintains the official state church of the God King, with grand cathedrals and a formal clergy who perform daily rites. For the nobility, attendance is often a public and political necessity. For the city's poor, the same services are often a sincere source of communal identity, although faith is not universal.

This state-sanctioned faith tends to dilute with distance, so that a connected industrial town like Loxmere may have a strong state church presence, but remote territories are more tenuous. In regions like the Foxhollow Plain on the Askaran border, or distant settlements like Greywold and Dunhollow, the state religion is little more than a rumor.

In these peripheral lands, communities honor their local gods, who are a tangible presence in their daily lives. These practices have more in common with Askaran folk belief than the high ceremony of Bellegrad, and a border village may even make offerings to Askaran deities to ensure a good harvest or safe passage.

### **Cities and Sites of Interest**

Bethune has continued to expand throughout its long and storied history, from a small kingdom, to the sprawling theocracy it is today. Many rural areas still bear remnants of the old kingdoms absorbed into Bethune, including abandoned keeps and crumbling fortifications. Bellegrad, the nation's heart, is surrounded by smaller settlements and factory towns that serve its growing industrial demands, while its distant borders are marked by military outposts and contested land.

#### **_Bellegrad_**

The sprawling capital of Bethune is the center of the nation's theocratic and industrial power. The city's life is dominated by The Kyrie, the God King's palace, and the seat of the Divine Council. Bellegrad is a city of policed stratification with residential districts like Aethelmere being walled off from the industrial hub of Wight's Row or the crime-ridden Venel Market. Order and access to many districts are maintained by the Peace Officers who handle common crime and the Plague Wardens, who enforce quarantines and the Council's will. Furthermore, the city's architecture has come to be shaped by recurring sickness, allowing most districts to be permanently gated and closed to prevent the spread of any new plague.

#### **_Loxmere_**

Loxmere is a fortified industrial town on the border with Steinlund. Its ironworks produce the weapons and rail components that fuel Bethune, but its proximity to Steinlund makes it an increasingly dangerous location. Soldiers stand guard, ostensibly to protect Bethunian interests, while overworked laborers push through shifts, both knowing that the Zwerge could attack to drive the Bethunians at any moment. Loxmere has the feel of a temporary town, designed to rob any wages earned through charlatans, brothels, and taverns before the resources there are stripped away.

#### **_Tovalan Keep_**

Tovalan Keep in the Grimswald Forest was once the linchpin of Bethune's defense against the ogres of Gane more than a century ago, when their aggression against Tuinleas required more attention. While its strategic importance has not faded due to Ibunor's occupation of Gane, the memory of that distant war has, leaving its stone walls in disrepair, ill prepared for conflicts that may still be on the horizon. Soldiers stationed here rarely see it as a stepping stone to greater glory, knowing it is, most often, a dead end, even if its proximity to Ibunese-occupied Gane makes it a potential flashpoint for spillover conflicts.

### **The Divine Council**

Of the thirteen noble families that sit on the Divine Council, five hold significant power, their identities forged by the recurring plagues and their control over a specific pillar of the Bethunian state.

House Aldridge is known for their alchemical and medicinal expertise, making them the Council's primary voice on experimental treatments and chemical science. House Beggley is known for its members' keen legal minds, who often dominate the Council's judicial and legal proceedings. House Compton built their legacy as stewards of national infrastructure after their vast estates were converted into quarantine zones during the Great Plague. House Griswold is an immensely wealthy family whose power comes from their extensive mining operations, which supply Bethune with precious metals and gemstones. House Henslowe manages Bethune's inland waterways, giving them control over most shipping and trade that flows through the nation's rivers and canals. House Lockridge are diplomats and advisors whose lineage traces back to the first Divine Council; they pride themselves on mediation and poise. House Marlowe is a historically minor house that recently gained immense power by marrying into an industrialist merchant family, giving them a near-monopoly on industrial labor. House Wainwright controls Bethune's agricultural wealth, leveraging its vast grain stores and control over the food supply to maintain its indispensable role on the Council.

#### **_House Barton_**

The Bartons are Bethune's hereditary generals, a family with a long martial legacy that has supplied commanders to the army since the God King's ascension. Their seat on the Council advocates for military solutions to all of the state's problems, both foreign and domestic. They consistently push for increased military funding and power, a hawkish stance that is a frequent source of tension with the other noble lines.

#### **_House Fairweather_**

The Fairweathers are the bankers of the aristocracy. They were ennobled after they used their immense wealth to stabilize Bethune's economy during the Black Vein plague. Their power comes from the debts owed to them by other noble houses and their control of capital. On the Council, they are a ruthless financial faction, protecting the interests of their clients and lobbying to divert state resources to the wealthiest districts.

#### **_House Kidham_**

House Kidham rose to prominence during the Second Death by organizing and commanding the first Plague Wardens. They have retained control of this state police force ever since. Their authority is absolute in matters of public health and enforcement, and their seat on the Council consistently tables draconian measures such as forced quarantines and relocations, giving them immense power over the populace during outbreaks.

#### **_House Pennington_**

An ancient house whose lineage traces back to the founding of Bellegrad, House Pennington's power is tied to their control of the nation's trade routes and merchant guilds. They are shrewd negotiators who leverage their mercantile dominance to dictate terms on all matters of commerce. Their seat on the Council is focused on securing trade and restricting the flow of goods to ensure maximum profit for their house.

#### **_House Wickham_**

House Wickham has been the steward of Bethune's religious institutions since the God King's ascension. Their power is derived from their control of the state church, its temples, and its clergy. On the Council, the Wickham seat acts as the voice of piety and diplomacy, often serving as an intermediary between the nobility and the commonfolk, a role that provides their family with immense popular goodwill and a subtle, pervasive influence.
