### **History**

During the early Bloom Age, the Dwergar founded the great city of Kizar Korath, the cultural and industrial heart of their civilization. For millennia they prospered, and to feed the insatiable appetite of their Great Forges, the mining settlement of Azare Kazak was established in the north, bound by a sacred "Brother's Oath."

The Blood Age brought a long and grinding war against the Cyclops of Baleros, cementing Kizar Korath's role as the Southern Anvil of the Dwergar people. While their forges armed the nation, a deep resentment took root as their northern kin in Azare Kazak grew culturally distant, treating oaths as business deals and disrespecting their shared heritage in favor of a cold, transactional worldview.

The schism erupted in the early Blight Age. Following a trade deal with Marcia, a diplomatic gift was properly delivered to the Drottin in Kizar Korath. Enraged by this customary show of respect, a Kazaki criminal committed treason, seizing the gift. When the Kazaki leaders refused to surrender the criminal, Kizar Korath dispatched its armies to restore the rule of law, an act that broke the Dwergar people in two.

Kizar Korath continues to stand as the bastion of Dwergar tradition, fighting a desperate three-front war. They face the Cyclops to the south, their traitorous kin to the north, and a secret, internal war against the corruption of the god Váttkorr and the emergence of the Gaospod Curse. Kizar Korath faces its darkest hour, fighting a secret, internal war against shapeshifters that have infiltrated every level of their society, threatening to consume the last bastion of Dwergar honor from within.

___

### **Government**

Kizar Korath is a traditional elective monarchy. The government is composed of a monarch, two legislative councils, and a system of appointed local officials. The Drottin (King) is the head of state, elected for life by the Thingheim (Assembly Home), the primary governing body composed of the hereditary Aettar-Jarl (Clan Chieftains). The Thingheim holds the sole power to elect the monarch and serves as the kingdom's highest legislative council. A lesser council, the Fylking (Throng), is composed of minor nobles and handles the administrative duties of the state, implementing the laws passed by the Thingheim. Throughout the monarchy, law is administered by appointed Dōmere (Magistrates) and enforced by Vordur (Marshals).

### **Interesting Facts**

- Despite Korath's narrative that personal merit is paramount, of Kizar Korath's six communal forges, only the Spire Forge is available to the common folk, while the rest are limited by station or faction. In addition, although artisans take pride in stamping their work with a personal maker's mark, the great clans have co-opted this right by forcing their most prized craftsmen to use the clan's sigil instead. Many of these artisans also embed a second, hidden maker's mark within their work to ensure their personal legacy endures.

- Common Dwergar use a system of patronymics or matronymics for their surname, while the great clans use static, hereditary surnames. This linguistic tradition makes a person's social class immediately identifiable by their name alone, reinforcing the rigid, caste-like nature of Korathi society, as any Dwergar without a clan name is immediately identified as being of a lower class.

- The political cold war between the great clans is fought through a system of espionage and political exile. Many of the clans maintain their own private intelligence networks to spy on each other and the king. It is also common practice for a powerful Jarl to secure a military command on the southern front for an ambitious rival, using a prestigious appointment to effectively banish them from the political machinations of the capital.

### **Current Challenges**

- **The Gaospod Curse.** Kizar Korath is besieged from within by the Gaospod, shapeshifting creatures spawned from the corruption of the god Váttkorr. These shapeshifters have infiltrated Korathi society, fostering widespread paranoia and eroding public trust. A specialized law enforcement unit, the Skugga-menn, has been established to hunt them, but their aggressive tactics and the difficulty of identifying Gaospod have led to accusations of wrongful persecution, further straining the social fabric.

- **The Traitor's War.** Korath is engaged in a persistent, low-intensity war with Kazak. The conflict is primarily one of espionage and sabotage, as the shared appearance and language of the two Dwergar peoples allow agents to move across borders with relative ease. This constant threat of infiltration requires a significant investment in counter-intelligence and internal security, diverting resources from other fronts.

- **The Southern Front.** Kizar Korath must maintain a costly, permanent military presence on its southern border to defend against the Cyclops of Baleros. This multi-millennial war of attrition drains the monarch's manpower and industrial resources. The need to garrison the south limits Korath's ability to fully commit its forces to the war with Kazak or the internal struggle against the Gaospod.

### **Religious Practices**

Korathi faith is a matter of public tradition and a source of national identity. They revere their gods, like Grimram and Váttkorr, and maintain numerous temples throughout their cities devoted to these deities. The kingdom's clergy conducts traditional rites and ceremonies, a practice that the Korathi view as a key cultural distinction from their rivals in Kazak.

The corruption of the god Váttkorr has, however, triggered a severe theological crisis that has destabilized the nation's religious life. Temples dedicated to Váttkorr have seen their congregations dwindle, with some being vandalized or abandoned entirely. The remaining clergy of the fallen god now face open hostility from a populace that blames them for the Gaospod curse. In the temples of other gods, sermons have become increasingly dogmatic, focusing on themes of unwavering faith and the damnation of heretics, while public theological debates often devolve into violent brawls between fervent traditionalists and a growing number of disillusioned skeptics.

### **Cities and Sites of Interest**

The Korathi Kingdom is located in the southern section of the mountains known as the Brotin Halm Fells. The nation's heartland is the Brotin Halm Fells, a range of resource-rich mountains that houses its capital, Kizar Korath. Its southern border is the Ori Balarika mountain range that extends into the nation of Baleros. The eastern lands near the Korath bay are humid and the west and southwest are arid grasslands and deserts.

#### **_Kizar Korath_**

The capital is a city carved into the heart of a mountain, accessible by a single, heavily guarded stone bridge. The city is vertically stratified into multiple levels. Many of the upper levels contain noble districts with the grand estates of the clans, temples, and the monarch's court, known as The Aerie. The middling districts are a series of cavernous tiers that house the city's markets, military garrison, and the massive, multi-level Spire Forge. The deepest or most inaccessible portions of the city are former quarries now crowded with the tenements of the working poor and clanless, and filled with the smoke and refuse from the forges above.

#### **_Glisméri_**

Glisméri \[glis-MER-ee] was once a pristine pool nestled at the base of the foothills of The Brotin Halm Fells, on the edge of a temperate rainforest. It is the sanctum for the Dwergar deity, Váttkorr, and is now a toxic mire where the water is thick with industrial runoff and rotting vegetation. The area is thought to be the origin point of the Gaospod Curse and is infested with these creatures. The blight is known to be actively expanding, slowly poisoning the surrounding lands.

#### **_Grjótvirki_**

Located in the red rock mountains of the Ori Balarika range, Grjótvirki \[GRYOTE-veer-kee] is a fortress complex built to contain the god, Orophylax. Its fortifications are carved directly into the mountainside, a series of sheer walls, siege emplacements, and massive iron gates that have been destroyed and rebuilt over centuries of warfare. The fortress is an active warzone, under periodic assault by the Cyclops of Baleros who seek to liberate their captive deity.

### **Factions of Korath**

The political landscape of Kizar Korath is divided by the conflict between two primary power structures: the traditional, hereditary authority of the great clans and the new monarch's regime. This struggle is further complicated by the emergence of the Skugga-menn, a secret police force loyal only to the crown.

#### **_Drottin_**

The institution of the monarchy serves as the traditional head of state in Kizar Korath. The current monarch, Einar Jarnhand, was elected by a partial assembly of the Thingheim during the start of the Gaospod crisis, when the previous monarch, Drottning Alvilde Lindbeck, was slain. He maintains his rule through the popular support of commoners who credit him with restoring order to the capital. He projects his authority through direct command of the state military and exclusive control of the Skugga-menn.

#### **_The Great Clans_**

The hereditary power of Kizar Korath resides in its great clans, whose Aettar-jarls are the voting members of the Thingheim. These families control the laws and the election of the monarch. Oftentimes, they recruit the most renowned artisans and vie for glory on the battlefield to gain social prestige. The clans are currently engaged in a political cold war over the monarch's legitimacy, with each Jarl using their influence within the Thingheim to protect their clan's assets and undermine their rivals.

Clan Hrafnfall is led by Jarl Lagertha, who was away on the southern border with Baleros during the Gaospod crisis. Her clan garrisons the monarchy's most heavily fortified border fortresses and supplies the majority of the army's professional soldiers. In the Thingheim, she uses her military authority to block the Drottin's initiatives and rally other traditionalist Jarls to her cause. Clan Jarnhand is the Drottin's own clan, elevated from minor status after his election. They were present in the capital during the Gaospod attack and were instrumental in organizing the vote that put him on the throne. They now use their royal patronage to install loyalists in key bureaucratic positions and seize control of Guild contracts from rival clans. Clan Steingard's primary asset is its vast wealth, derived from its control of the kingdom's richest silver and gem mines. Its Jarl, Vorlag, was absent from the capital during the Gaospod crisis. He now uses his financial power to fund dissenters and bribe officials within the Fylking, seeking to destabilize the king's rule through economic means.

#### **_Skugga-menn_**

The Skugga-menn are a state-funded secret police force created by Drottin Einar. They recruit from Dwergar who have lost family to the Gaospod, fostering a fanatical loyalty to the monarch. Operating under the official mandate of hunting shapeshifters, they have been granted broad authority by the crown to conduct raids, interrogations, and summary executions, often targeting political opponents of the Drottin under the guise of state security.
