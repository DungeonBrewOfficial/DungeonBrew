### **History**

During the long peace of the Bloom Age, the lands of Marcia were divided into distinct human cultures formed around local deities. They had little contact with other cultures, being mostly content with their local deities and customs. Avid sailors, they came to know the Long Deep, and the existence of Oskara, Sharasa, Kimara, and Acadiana, though it is not until the Blood Age that they sought out the peoples who inhabited these lands.

During the Blood Age, infighting among the various tribes within their Anterran lands began to arise, and the first kingdoms formed. Early kingdoms began to raid the shores of Sharasa, Askara, and Acadiana. Frequently they came to blows with the Kapre of Gialar, and slowly, the fragmented kingdoms began to consolidate. Late in the Blood Age, a single autocratic state formed from the kingdoms known as the Marcian Empire. At its height, the empire sought to conquer the human lands across the Long Deep, coming to rule a swath of the Oskaran coast, sacking the great city of Bakarash, and rebuilding it as the Anterran capital of Bellegrad. An eastern kingdom arose in 2,580 BdA, which would eventually take Bellegrad from Marcian hands, forming the nation of Bethune and driving the Marcians back to Anterra. After the loss of Bellegrad, Marcia suffered a series of defeats, losing control of all Oskaran holdings.

The Blight Age has been a slow decline for Marcia as the loss of their Oskaran territories triggered a political and economic upheaval. Refusing to accept their diminished status, successive rulers engaged in costly attempts to project their old power. They launched draining wars against the Kapre of Gialar and turned to massive loans from Acadiana to fund a rapid, debt-fueled industrialization. This combination of poor statecraft and failed campaigns created a bloated and inefficient bureaucracy to manage these desperate ventures, prolonging the empire's decay.

In the modern era, Marcia is a nation burdened by debt, struggling to remain relevant on the world stage, while it embroils itself in a war against Gialar to reaffirm its power. The state-enforced narrative of enduring greatness and an inevitable resurgence is hollow for the lower classes mired in industrial servitude in the crumbling tenements of their fading capital, Kavesk. The disparity between these desperate workers and the ruling Dvoryanstvo has created an internal crisis for anger and rebellion that is rapidly coming to a head.

___

### **Government**

Marcia is an autocratic monarchy, ruled by the Great King or Great Queen, known as the Velikiy Kral or Velikiy Kraljica. Political power is held exclusively by the noble class, the Dvoryanstvo, and is concentrated within the royal court, known as the Dvor. The government itself functions based on patronage within the Dvoryanstvo, which is divided into the court nobles, known as the Boylari, and the provincial nobles known as the Zupari who reside on their holdings and seek access to the court. At the apex of the Dvor is the Rada, a council of the most influential Boylari who hold the great offices of state and oftentimes the ear of the Great King or Great Queen.

### **Interesting Facts**

- At the Dvor, it is a tradition for lesser nobles to wear a sash or ribbon of a specific color to publicly declare their allegiance to a powerful Boylari or member of the Rada. The shifting palette of colors at any court function is a direct visual representation of the court's current political landscape, though such displays are often a bluff to test loyalties or mislead rivals.

- Once a year, the Velikiy Kral sponsors a grand lottery in which a single Kholopi family is granted its freedom and elevated to the status of Breguri. This is a massive public spectacle, a piece of political theater designed to provide a sliver of hope to the serf class and reinforce the Kral's image as the ultimate font of all mercy and status. However, this act is always a political move to select a specific family and is never random.

- The highest positions within the Marcian army are held exclusively by the Dvoryanstvo. However, it is common for noble officers to remain in fortified command posts while sending lower level Breguri officers to lead the most dangerous engagements. The Breguri and Kholopi fight and die while the noble takes the credit for any success.

### **Current Challenges**

- **The Gialar War.** The ongoing war with Gialar to acquire resources within the Hanay Batong Buhay Mountains has become a prolonged, grinding conflict against an intractable enemy. For the Marcian State, it has become a matter of pride, as they weigh the investiture versus reward of acquiring this region. Each day brings losses and more debt that the state can ill afford.

- **The Acadian Debt-Trap.** To fund its wars and industry, Marcia has fallen into crippling debt to Acadian financiers. Still operating as the imperial power it once was has created a spiral of debt that has exacerbated economic tensions within the nation. Marcia has entered into a form of economic vassalage, with Acadian interests quietly influencing the nation's politics and controlling key industries, debasing any faith its people have in the strength of their government.

- **The Zaharmak Sedition.** The revolutionary faction Zaharmak has taken deep root within the disenfranchised Kholopi of Kavesk. The slave-workers have become increasingly restless as they move from the fields to the factories, experiencing extended and brutal conditions that leave many sick or injured. Now, saboteurs and radicals have resulted in the streets of the industrial districts of Kavesk becoming bloodbaths. Many Kholopi are faced with the decision of joining the faction or facing their indiscriminate wrath.

### **Religious Practices**

Religious life in Marcia is bound to the state church, The Patriarchate of Kavesk, led by the Slovnik, a powerful noble who sits on The Rada. The church's primary function is to interpret the myths of the nation's many local deities to serve the interests of the state. The central doctrine enforced by the Slovnik and their priests is to teach that all beings have a proper place, either as master or as servant.

This ideology is most famously illustrated through the state-sanctioned parable of the sea goddess Bryshteya and her sister, Zabyt'ya. In the church's official telling, Bryshteya is the powerful, dominant master of the seas, and Zabyt'ya is the perfect, devoted servant whose selfless acts of loyalty are held up as the ultimate ideal for the common classes. Through such stories, the church provides a divine justification for the Dvoryanstvo's rule and the Kholopi's servitude, making religious practice an extension of state propaganda.

### **Cities and Sites of Interest**

Marcia is a temperate, wet nation on the northwest coast of Anterra. Its lands are covered in deep woodlands, from the Syvash Pushcha in the south to the Chornolissia Forest in the north. Hemmed in by the Hanay Batong Buhay Mountains of Gialar to the east and the Tregintir Wastes of Khunland to the south, its civilization is centered on its western coast, where its capital sits on a narrow peninsula along the Long Deep.

#### **_Kavesk_**

The capital of Marcia is a city of grand but decaying imperial architecture, now choked by the soot and sprawl of debt-fueled factories and crumbling tenements. Kavesk is beset by a strange and accelerating decay; the newest industrial district suffers from a rash of unexplained structural collapses, outbreaks of a wasting sickness, and reports of monstrous, ooze-like creatures emerging from its sewer grates. It is not a secret that the district is dangerous, but the single, underlying cause of these phenomena remains unknown.

#### **_The Syvash Pushcha_**

This southern marsh-forest extends to the east to Marcia's border with Gialar. The Pushcha serves as the primary staging ground for the ongoing war with the Kapre. Its dense, swampy terrain is riddled with hidden military outposts and skirmishes between Marcian patrols and Kapre guerilla fighters are frequent. This region provides one of the most direct routes into Gialar through the low-lying foothills into the Paglubog Buhangin Desert.

#### **_The Chornolissia Forest_**

Known as the Black Forest, this woodland is the sanctum of the reclusive goddess, Zabyt'ya. The forest's isolation has made it an ideal location for a hidden Zaharmak outpost. From this concealed base, rebel cells sail across the Morye Tyshi Sea to launch attacks against industrial targets in Kavesk. The constant rebel activity, and the state's increasingly aggressive patrols to hunt them down, both serve to threaten the stability of Zabyt'ya's sanctum.

### **Marcian Castes**

The castes of the Marcian state have limited permeability, often preventing significant movement in either direction. As such, movement between classes and positions is based on favor or disfavor from those of a higher position. The Kholopi may try to gain freedom through military service so they can become Breguri. A Breguri may attempt to marry into a Zupari family by offering princely gifts. A Zupari may attempt to get an invitation to court by providing numerous Kholopi for a military engagement. A Boylari may attempt to gain position on the Rada by exposing the corruption of a current member and gaining the favor of the Velikiy Kral.

#### **_The Velikiy Kral / Kraljica_**

The Velikiy Kral \[Veh-LEE-KEE KRALL] or Kraljica \[kral-YEE-tsa] is the absolute autocrat of Marcia, the sole font from which all rank and authority are granted. Over the centuries, some monarchs have ruled with an iron fist, others have cultivated rivalries, and others have amounted to no more than puppets to be played by the Dvoryanstvo. As a result, time has created a sprawling system of state offices, both redundant and essential, but ultimately sought after to gain both prestige and the ear of the monarch.

#### **_The Dvoryanstvo_**

The Dvoryanstvo \[dvo-RYAN-stvuh] is the hereditary ruling class of Marcia, a stratified nobility who compete for power and the monarch's favor.

- The Rada \[Ra-DAH] is the inner circle of the Dvor, composed of more than a dozen Boylari who have curried favor from the monarch, whether through sycophancy, skill, wealth, or prestige. They wield immense power, but their positions are the primary targets of the ambitions of all other court nobles.

- The Boylari \[BOW-lar-EE] are the established court nobles who have secured a place at the Dvor \[Dvohr]. They command provincial clients, drawing upon their support in the form of levies and resources. Their primary focus is a constant game of political maneuvering and intrigue, aimed at undermining their rivals to secure a coveted position on The Rada.

- The Zupari \[ZHU-par-EE] are the provincial nobles who reside outside the Dvor and are beholden to their patron, one of the Boylari. They endeavor to be recognized by the Boylari, Rada, or Kral to gain favor and, ideally, an invitation to court. They are the foundation of the nobility's power, yet they are the furthest removed from it, constantly at risk of being replaced by a more promising rival.

#### **_The Breguri_**

The Breguri \[bre-GOO-ri] are a small and legally ambiguous class of freemen, existing outside the formal power structure. This diverse group is composed of disgraced or landless nobles, the descendants of liberated military veterans, and successful merchants who have accumulated wealth in the cities. While they possess personal freedom, they have no formal political power and are viewed with contempt by the Dvoryanstvo, who may nonetheless be forced to deal with them for their wealth or specialized skills.

#### **_The Kholopi_**

The Kholopi \[khah-LOH-pee] are the vast, unfree serf class that forms the foundation of the entire Marcian state. Bound by law to the land, a factory, or military service, they are considered the property of the noble houses. Their labor is the ultimate source of the resources and levies that fuel the ambitions of the Dvoryanstvo, yet they have no rights and no hope of advancement beyond the exceptionally rare chance of being granted freedom for extraordinary service to the state.
