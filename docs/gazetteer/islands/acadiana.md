### **History**

The Lutin of Acadiana trace their ancestral connection to the Tonttu of Oskara during the Bloom Age, with many competing myths of their people and their origins. And though they squabble over the specifics, tradition also holds that a close friendship was forged with several nearby Ogre tribes. It is said that this is why their own language is so similar to that of the Ogres, though whether they adopted it from the Ogres or vice versa is a matter of some debate, especially when discussing how their culture is distinct from their Tonttu kin.

Early in the Blood Age, legend tells of the Lutin-Ogre coalition in which they fought side-by-side in the great Ogre civil wars, which ended in their eventual defeat. The survivors, the Lutin and the last of their Ogre allies, fled their ancestral lands and crossed the Long Deep, arriving on the island of Acadiana weakened and depleted, only to be conquered by the rising kingdoms of Marcia, which made the island a colonial holding. After a long period of Marcian occupation, the Lutin and the now-native Ogres successfully repelled their masters late in the Blood Age and reclaimed their independence.

Throughout the early centuries of the Blight Age, the now-independent nation focused on rebuilding its strength and consolidating power on the island. It was during this period of growth that Acadiana's wealthiest merchants founded The Navtog, a guild created to secure and expand their maritime trade interests. It is from this founding faction that they became the dominant power in the Long Deep.

Now, Acadiana exerts its influence across the Long Deep through the fleets of The Navtog, which has grown into a vast economic and military empire. Its prosperity is built on colonial ventures and the slave trade. The nation is maintained by a system of public welfare and civic spectacles, all funded by the wealth of an empire forged from defeat, determined never to be subjugated again.

___

### **Government**

Acadiana is a plutocratic republic governed by a large assembly known as the Lasanble Souverèn. The assembly is composed of Syndics, who are representatives from the nation's wealthiest merchant and noble families. The official function of the Lasanble is to debate policy and pass legislation for the nation.

The Lasanble is presided over by a Speaker, elected from among the Syndics for a two-year term. The Speaker is supported by three Deputy Speakers, who are also elected from the assembly for four-year terms. Resolutions are brought before the Lasanble after being signed by a required number of Syndics and are passed by a majority vote. A Speaker can be removed from office by the agreement of two of the three Deputy Speakers.

### **Interesting Facts**

- The grand arena in Keolarus is officially named the Teyat Viktwa (Theater of Victory). Among the Jweyo (The Players) who perform there, however, it is known only by the colloquial name Le Sèk (The Circle). Some enjoy the glory and take pride in their position, others gall under the whips of their masters within the bloody sport, rebelling with their language, even if they cannot rebel with their actions.

- Acadian high society is governed by Kòd la (The Code), a complex and unwritten set of social rules. While not official law, this code of honor dictates all matters of proper etiquette, from formal greetings to the conduct of duels, and has a direct legal impact. In any civil dispute brought before the Lasanble, the testimony of an individual from a family with high standing in Kòd la is legally weighted as being more credible than that of a commoner.

- While the Ogre minority is integrated into all levels of Acadian society, including the nobility, the position of Speaker of the Lasanble Souverèn has always been held by a Lutin. It is an unspoken tradition that, while Ogres are valued for their commercial acumen and straightforward dealings, the highest leadership role requires a capacity for the intricate, often duplicitous, political maneuvering that is considered beyond an Ogre's capacity.

### **Current Challenges**

- **The Navtog's Shadow.** The transnational power of The Navtog now rivals, and in many ways exceeds, that of Acadiana. The organization operates largely above Acadian law, and a quiet power struggle is brewing between the Lasanble Souverèn and the High Commodore of The Navtog over who truly controls the nation's destiny.

- **The Rot Within.** The Lasanble Souverèn is a bloated, corrupt, and fragmented body, incapable of passing meaningful legislation or responding effectively to crises. This institutional paralysis is the nation's greatest internal weakness, as the powerful factions would rather see the state fail than cede any of their own power to a rival.

- **The Failing Containment.** The massive, centuries-old engineering projects that imprison the island's enraged Tatarigami are beginning to fail. The constant, costly maintenance is becoming unsustainable, and the risk of a catastrophic breach by an entity like Papa Fango is a growing existential threat to the nation. More and more coin is being poured into these works, giving rise to the question of whether more permanent options should be explored.

### **Acadian Characters**

As you create an Acadian character or NPC, consider the following:

**The Kòd la.** The social rules that guide polite society within Acadiana are a top-down cultural norm that lower classes are expected to abide by when engaging with upper classes. Do you value the social etiquette of The Code? Have you experienced the legal or social impacts of how others perceive you based on The Code? Do you believe The Code reflects personal or societal honor?

**A Founding Legacy.** Acadiana was founded by an alliance of two peoples, the Lutin and the Ogre. To which people do you belong? Do you see your two peoples as being equal? Do you believe that the society of Acadiana is a reflection of your shared legacy?

**The Price of Prosperity.** The line between citizen and property is a matter of debt. Every path to wealth and power in Acadiana is controlled by a monopolistic faction or society. Do you believe that a person's wealth is a measure of their quality? Do you believe that your people have a right to control those they capture and put into bondage? How are you hindered or benefited by this system?

### **Religious Practices**

Religious life in Acadiana is divided between the state-controlled system that manages the island's more dangerous divine entities, and the more localized priesthoods that engage in worship. The threats of Tatarigami, such as Papa Fango and Bwa Tòde, are contained out of necessity through ongoing engineering and culling operations by secular societies like La Sosyete Mason.

Meanwhile, for deities like Limyè Kole, religious rites are not intruded upon by the Lasanble Souverèn. Throughout Acadiana, the informal, local priesthoods are free to practice their religion with their devotees, relying on donations, rather than any official sanction from the government.

### **Cities and Sites of Interest**

Acadiana is a temperate island nation of sprawling wetlands, dense forests, and rocky coastlines, frequently battered by storms from the Long Deep. The nation's lifeblood is the sea, with its grand capital of Keolarus and the formidable naval fortress of Pòtay la dominating the coasts. In the island's interior, the landscape has been reshaped by the massive containment projects to imprison the island's native gods.

#### **_Keolarus_**

Keolarus is the grand capital of Acadiana and the seat of the Lasanble Souverèn. The city is known for its opulent, state-funded architecture and the constant festivals that serve to distract the populace. It is a city of extreme contrasts, where the immense wealth of the great families is on constant display against the backdrop of the impoverished underclass that serves them. The central feature is the Great Arena, in which slaves fight to entertain the masses.

#### **_Gran Marè_**

The Gran Marè (Great Marsh) is a vast swamp in the island's interior that serves as the containment site for the Tatarigami Papa Fango. The entire region is enclosed by a system of dams and channels to contain the enraged god. The engineers of La Sosyete Mason maintain the prison, but have no means of confronting the deity itself, should it target the prison itself. Slaves are often employed to distract the deity so the engineers can complete their work.

#### **_Pòtay la_**

Pòtay la (The Gateway) is the formidable naval fortress and primary shipyard of The Navtog. Built within a natural bay on the island's southeast coast, the port is a city of industry, the forges and shipyards that build and maintain the Acadian fleets, and massive slave pens. It is the heart of the nation's maritime power, a symbol of their dominance over the Long Deep.

### **Lasanble Souverèn**

Political power in Acadiana is held by competing societies and factions. The Lasanble Souverèn (Sovereign Assembly) is the forum where their representatives meet, but true authority is wielded by the factions themselves, each a legally sanctioned monopoly in its own right.

#### **_San Ansyen an_**

San Ansyen an (The Ancient Blood) is a coalition of true noble Lutin and Ogre families who claim the right to rule through lineage. Their power is largely derived through hereditary land holdings on the island. They use their cultural and social authority to influence policy, often acting as a conservative check on the purely profit-driven ambitions of the newer societies.

#### **_Sosyete Machann yo_**

Sosyete Machann yo (The Merchant's Society) is a coalition of the great Lutin and Ogre merchant houses. Their wealth and influence are derived from their control over the domestic industries of Acadiana, such as manufacturing, agriculture, and the distribution of finished goods within the nation. They are the masters of the internal economy, and they leverage this control to secure favorable contracts and legislation from the Lasanble.

#### **_Sendika Fè a_**

Sendika Fè a (The Syndicate of Iron) controls the domestic slave trade, and is largely composed of native Ogre merchants. This faction manages every stage of the process, from the legal conversion of debtors into property to the distribution of foreign captives supplied by The Navtog. They operate the slave auctions that supply the nation's labor force, soldiers, gladiators for training, and are an indispensable component of the Acadian economy.

#### **_Chenn Lò a_**

Chenn Lò a (The Golden Chain) is a league of bankers and creditors who control the nation's capital. Their power is wielded through the loans and leveraged debt they extend to all other factions, from noble houses refinancing their estates to merchant societies funding new ventures. They operate as the silent partners in every major enterprise, using their financial influence to dictate terms and profit from every transaction.

#### **_Lekòl la_**

The spectacle of the gladiatorial games is the domain of Lekòl la (The School). This coalition operates the schools and institutions where captives and debtors are conditioned into performers for the arena. As the sole producers of the entertainment that keep the Acadian masses placated, they hold significant social power and are patronized by all the other great factions.

#### **_La Kominote_**

La Kominote (The Community) is a collection of smaller, specialized guilds, or Sosyetes, that control specific sectors of the economy. The masters of each Sosyete hold a position as a Syndic within the Lasanble. While their individual interests often conflict, their sheer number makes them a sizable force, capable of forming temporary alliances to advance or obstruct legislation that affects their respective trades. Some societies include La Sosyete Pechè (The Fishers' Society), La Sosyete Bòs Fòjon (The Smiths' Society), La Sosyete Bòs Chapant (The Carpenters' Society), La Sosyete Tiseran (The Weavers' Society), La Sosyete Bouche (The Butchers' Society), and La Sosyete Mason (The Masons' Society).
