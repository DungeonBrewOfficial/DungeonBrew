## Cleric: Corruption Domain

*Spread and Endure the Rot*

<div class="page-split-equal" markdown="1">

<div class="sidebar-content" markdown="1">

Clerics of the Corruption Domain wield the forces of decay, turning the power of entropy against their enemies. These clerics thrive on weakening others, eroding their strength, and hastening the inevitable collapse of order.

The Corruption Domain is tied to gods of disease, destruction, and vengeance. These deities view decay as a necessary force in the cycle of existence, tearing down to make way for renewal. Clerics of this domain are often feared and shunned, yet their power is undeniable in breaking down what is stagnant or oppressive.

### Level 3: Corrupting Curse

As a Magic action, you present your holy symbol and expend one use of your Channel Divinity to curse one creature that you can see within 30 feet of you. The curse lasts for 1 minute, or until you lose your concentration (as if concentrating on a spell).

- The target's movement speed is reduced by half.
- At the start of each of its turns, the target takes Necrotic damage equal to a number of d6s equal to your proficiency bonus. The target must make a Constitution saving throw against your cleric spell save DC. On a successful save, it takes half as much of this Necrotic damage.

### Level 3: Corruption Domain Spells

Your connection to this divine domain ensures you always have certain spells ready. When you reach a Cleric level specified in the Corruption Domain Spells table, you thereafter always have the listed spells prepared.

| Corruption Domain Spells | |
|:---:|:---:|
| **Cleric Level** | **Spells** |
| 3 | *Bane, Blindness/Deafness, Defiled Ground, Inflict Wounds* |
| 5 | *Bestow Curse, Stinking Cloud* |

### Level 3: Festering Rot

When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, or when a creature takes damage from one of your cleric spell attacks, the attack deals an additional 1d8 Poison damage to that creature, and the target can't regain hit points until the end of your next turn.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (a minimum of once). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

### Level 6: Improved Corrupting Curse

If a creature dies while affected by your Corrupting Curse feature, its body explodes in a burst of ichor. Each creature of your choice within 5 feet of the dying creature must make a Dexterity saving throw against your cleric spell save DC. On a failed save, a creature takes Necrotic damage equal to one roll of your Corrupting Curse damage dice (this explosion damage is not halved by a save).

Additionally, if a creature fails its saving throw against this explosion, you can choose for that creature to become cursed as if affected by your Corrupting Curse feature. This new application of the curse lasts for its full duration, requires your concentration, and does not expend an additional use of your Channel Divinity.

### Level 17: Eternal Curse

As a Magic action, you can release a palpable aura of sickness and decay. This miasma extends in a 60-foot radius from you, lasts for 1 minute, or until you choose to end it (no action required).

While the miasma is active, you gain the following benefits:

- Creatures of your choice within the miasma have disadvantage on saving throws against your Corrupting Curse feature and against any spell you cast that deals poison or necrotic damage.
- When you roll poison or necrotic damage for a cleric spell or Channel Divinity effect against a target within the miasma, you can choose to change the damage type to the other (poison becomes necrotic, or necrotic becomes poison).

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (a minimum of once). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

## Cleric: Madness Domain

*Embrace the knowledge in chaos*

The madness domain represents those who have looked into the abyss and survived... mostly. There are many magics available to those that risk their own minds. Now, having come through the darkness, they can reach back to learn. Or bring some of the darkness here to influence the minds of others.

### Level 3: Disruptive Burst

As a Magic action, you present your holy symbol and expend one use of your Channel Divinity to release a burst of psychic energy. Choose any number of creatures you can see within 30 feet of you. Each chosen creature must make a Wisdom saving throw against your cleric spell save DC. On a failed save, the creature can take only an action or a bonus action on its next turn, not both. Any creature that fails its saving throw by 5 or more gains the Stunned condition until the end of its next turn.

### Level 3: Hidden Knowledge

Your otherworldly connection has taught many lessons in obscure arcane theory, and in reading discord in the minds of others. You gain advantage on Intelligence (Arcana) checks and Wisdom (Insight) checks.

### Level 3: Madness Domain Spells

Your connection to this divine domain ensures you always have certain spells ready. When you reach a Cleric level specified in the Madness Domain Spells table, you always have the listed spells prepared.

| Madness Domain Spells | |
|:---:|:---:|
| **Cleric Level** | **Spells** |
| 3 | *Comprehend Languages, Hideous Laughter, Crown of Madness, Enthrall* |
| 5 | *Hunger, Tongues* |
| 7 | *Confusion, Arcane Eye* |
| 9 | *Contact Other Plane, Dominate Person* |

### Level 6: Muttering Madness

As a Bonus action, you can choose one creature you can see within 30 feet of you and assault its mind with discordant energies. The creature has disadvantage on the next Wisdom, Intelligence, or Charisma saving throw it makes against one of your spells or Channel Divinity effects before the end of your next turn.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

### Level 17: Mind of Pandemonium

Your mind is in tune with chaos, causing it to be both a shield and weapon. You gain resistance to psychic damage. Additionally, you are immune to the Charmed and Frightened conditions.

Additionally, you always have the Feeblemind spell prepared, and it doesn't count against the number of spells you can prepare each day.

## Fighter: Irregular

*From elite combat units, to criminal enforcers. Irregulars represent those who have become frighteningly familiar with firearms.*

!!! warning "Note: Two Versions"

    The source document contains two versions of the Irregular subclass. Both are included below. The first is more focused on firearm mechanics (misfire, scatter, ammunition types). The second uses a Tactical Toolkit resource system. Review and choose which version to keep.

### Version A: Firearm Specialist

#### Level 3: Maintain your weapon

You gain proficiency with Tinker's tools. You can do the required maintenance to reduce your firearms Misfire Number in 1 minute regardless of how high the Misfire Number is.

#### Level 3: Weapon Versatility

You have trained with all types of firearms, gaining the following benefits:

- When rolling damage for a weapon with the Scatter property, you may reroll any dice that rolls a 1.
- If you hit a creature with a ranged attack roll using a firearm with the Two-Handed property, you can make a ranged attack roll against a second creature with this weapon in a straight line directly behind the first and within the weapon's normal range. On a hit, the second creature takes damage equal to your ability modifier.
- When you attack with a firearm that lacks the Two-handed property, you can replace its mastery property with the Ricochet property for that attack.
- If you have a loaded firearm ready, you can use it to make Opportunity Attacks. This attack must only target one creature, and follows normal rules for shooting in melee.

#### Level 7: Muscle Memory

Repetition in training means speed in the field. You can clear a jammed firearm using a Bonus Action. You can also stow a weapon and draw another in the same item interaction. Also, any Tinker's tools checks you make to repair your firearms are made with Advantage.

#### Level 10: Improved Weapon Versatility

You gain even more options while wielding firearms. You may only use one Weapon Versatility option per attack.

- When you fire a firearm that has the Scatter property, if there is only one creature within the range, it has Disadvantage on the Dexterity save to avoid damage.
- While wielding a firearm with the Two-handed property, you can use a Bonus Action to make a melee attack with the stock of the weapon. The weapon deals Bludgeoning damage, and the weapon's damage die for this attack is a d4.
- If you are the target of a ranged attack while holding a loaded firearm that lacks the Two-Handed property, you may use your Reaction to fire towards the attacker causing the attack to have Disadvantage.

#### Level 15: Enhanced Ammunition

You have learned how to modify your firearms to shoot non-standard ammunition. You may change the ammunition type on a firearm at the end of a long rest. You may have Intelligence mod firearms modified at once.

- Incendiary: Fire damage
- Cryo: Cold damage
- Concussive: Thunder damage
- Corrosive: Acid damage

#### Level 18: Bullet Storm

On a turn where you use an Action Surge, you may reload a Long-Load weapon as a Bonus Action. Also during this turn, your guns will only jam on a natural 1.

### Version B: Tactical Toolkit

#### Level 3: Field Operative

You gain proficiency with Tinker's Tools. You also gain proficiency in one of the following skills: Acrobatics, Athletics, Intimidation, Perception, Stealth.

#### Level 3: Tactical Toolkit

You have learned to blend various combat actions into a fluid sequence, allowing you to stow one weapon and draw another as a single item interaction on your turn.

**Inertia.** You can use one of the following Tactical Toolkit options a number of times equal to half your Fighter level (rounded up). You regain two expended uses when you finish a Short Rest and all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

- **Juggling Attack.** Once per turn, when you take the Attack action, and you swap one weapon you are wielding for another, you can expend one use of Inertia to make one additional weapon attack with the newly drawn weapon as part of the same action.
- **Momentum.** Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a melee weapon as part of the Attack action, you can expend one use of Inertia to force a Strength saving throw to Disarm or Push the target or a Dexterity saving throw to Trip the target. You gain an additional 10 feet of movement until the end of your turn.
- **Reposition.** On your turn, you can expend one use of Inertia to take one of the following actions as a Bonus Action: Dash, Disengage, or Dodge.

**Saving Throws.** The target must be Large or smaller and the DC equals 8 plus your Strength or Dexterity modifier (your choice) and Proficiency Bonus.

#### Level 7: Advanced Toolkit

You have expanded your knowledge of unconventional tactics. The following options are added to the menu for your Tactical Toolkit:

**Quick Draw.** Once per turn, when you swap from a melee weapon to a ranged weapon, you gain the Deadly or Ricochet Mastery until the end of your turn.

**Contact.** Once per turn, when you swap from a ranged weapon to a melee weapon, you gain the Graze or Topple Mastery until the end of your turn.

**Recover.** Once per turn, when you use your Reaction, you can expend one use of Inertia to use a second Reaction.

#### Level 10: Muscle Memory

As a Bonus Action, you can do one of the following.

**Field Strip.** Make a Dexterity or Intelligence (Tinker's Tools) check to unjam a weapon.

**Use an Object.** Take the Utilize action, or take the Magic action to use a magic item that requires that action.

#### Level 15: Dismantle

When you hit a creature with your Juggling Attack or Momentum Inertia, the target must make a Wisdom saving throw or be Stunned until the end of your next turn.

#### Level 18: Salvo

When you use your Action Surge, one firearm of your choice reloads and you regain all your expended Inertia.

## Monk: Combat Medic

*From suturing wounds, to the latest chemical enhancement, you have been thoroughly trained on how to keep a squad alive and moving.*

### Level 3: Locked and Stocked

You gain proficiency in Medicine checks. During a short rest, you can gather supplies from your surroundings. If you choose this option you can either create a new Healer's kit, or restore spent uses to a Healer's kit you are carrying.

### Level 3: First Aid

As a Utilize action, you can expend a use from a carried Healer's kit to bind the wounds of a creature that you can touch. While doing so you can expend Focus points to heal the creature by an amount equal to two rolls of your martial arts die per Focus point. You can spend a maximum Focus points equal to your Wisdom Bonus.

### Level 6: Concoctions

From well fed leeches reduced to injectable liquids, to pastes made with plenty of poppy flowers. These Concoctions represent the best modern medicine has to offer.

These effects last for 10 minutes. You may prepare Wisdom Bonus doses at the end of a Short Rest, and may only have that many created simultaneously. It takes an Action to administer Concoctions.

If administered by anyone other than the Combat Medic who created it, then it takes a DC15 Medicine check as well. On a failed check the Concoction was administered incorrectly and is used with no effect.

1. Speed — Increase your movement speed by 15 feet.
2. Adrenaline — You gain advantage on Strength saves and checks.
3. Boost — You gain 5 temporary hit points at the start of each turn.
4. Cleanse — End any Poisoned or Diseased condition.
5. Calming — Immune to Fear.
6. Stimulant — Advantage on Investigation and Perception checks.

### Level 11: Take your Time

You can spend your Short Rest tending to yourself and any allies who are also resting. If you choose to do so, expend one use from your Healer's kit per creature you wish to treat. Roll a Medicine check, each creature is restored an amount of Hit points equal to the result.

### Level 17: Improved Concoctions

These represent some of the absolute cutting edge of medical science. You may create Wisdom Bonus doses at the end of a Long Rest, and may only have that many created simultaneously. These are separate from your allotment of regular Concoctions.

1. Tonic — Cure Exhaustion Level.
2. Restorative — Cure any reduction of Ability Scores or Hit Points.
3. Regenerative — Regenerate lost body parts. Creature must be alive.
4. Pain Blocking — Resistance to Bludgeoning, Slashing, and Piercing damage.
5. Mental Protection — Advantage on Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saves.
6. Steroids — 1d8 per melee hit.

## Rogue: Demolitionist

*Precise Destruction*

Demolitionists blend a rogue's cunning with a dangerous affinity for explosives. They are experts in the construction and tactical deployment of bombs, turning volatile substances into tools of precise destruction. Whether disarming a complex trap or breaching a fortified wall, the Demolitionist brings a unique and explosive brand of expertise to any operation.

### Level 3: Demolitionist's Tools

You gain Alchemist's Supplies and Tinker's Tools, and you have proficiency with them.

### Level 3: The Basics

You have learned to craft rudimentary explosives. When you finish a short rest, if you spend that rest crafting, you can create a number of bombs equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of one bomb). You can have a maximum number of these bombs equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of one).

As an action, you can light and throw one of these bombs at a point you choose within 30 feet. Each creature within a 5-foot radius sphere centered on that point must make a Dexterity saving throw. A creature takes Fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. The number of d6s rolled for this Fire damage is equal to the number of d6s you roll for your Sneak Attack at your current rogue level. The saving throw DC is calculated as 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier.

When you create your bombs, you can choose to imbue them with one of your known Cunning Strike options. To do so, you reduce the number of Fire damage dice the bomb deals by the die cost specified in the Cunning Strike option. This decision is made when the bomb is created.

### Level 9: Getting Tricky

You learn the following Cunning Strike options, which you can apply to your bombs by forgoing a certain number of the bomb's Fire damage dice, as specified by the option's cost.

- **Sticky Bomb (Cost: 1d6).** The bomb is covered in sticky tar. If you target a creature with the bomb, make a ranged attack roll against the target (you are proficient with these bombs and use your Dexterity modifier for the attack roll). On a hit, the bomb adheres to the target, and the target does not make a Dexterity saving throw to halve the bomb's damage. To use this effect, you must have Alchemist's Supplies on your person when crafting the bomb.
- **Smoke Bomb (Cost: 1d6).** The bomb creates a 5-foot radius sphere of smoke centered on the point of detonation. The area within the smoke is heavily obscured. The smoke lasts until the end of your next turn.
- **Grease Bomb (Cost: 1d6).** A creature that fails its Dexterity saving throw against the bomb is also covered in flammable grease and has the Burning condition for 1 minute. The creature can use its action to extinguish the flames.
- **Trigger Bomb (Cost: 2d6).** You can add either a tripwire or a timer to your bomb. If you choose a tripwire, a creature can spot the tripwire with a successful Wisdom (Perception) check against your bomb save DC. The bomb detonates when the tripwire is disturbed. If you choose a timer, you can set it to detonate at the end of a specific turn, up to a number of minutes equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of 1 minute). To use this effect, you must have Tinker's Tools on your person when crafting the bomb.

### Level 13: Powder Aficionado

You have become adept at maximizing the destructive potential of your bombs.

- Your bombs deal double damage to objects and structures.
- Fire damage from your bombs ignores resistance to Fire damage.

### Level 17: More Options

You learn additional ways to modify your bombs and new Cunning Strike options for them.

1. **Efficient Construction (Cost: 1d6 per).** When you create a bomb, you can choose to reduce the number of its Fire damage dice. For each damage die you forgo in this way, the bomb's saving throw DC increases by 1 to a maximum bonus equal to your Intelligence modifier.
2. **Shrapnel Bomb (Cost: 3d6).** A creature that fails its Dexterity saving throw against the bomb also takes additional piercing damage equal to half of the Fire damage they took from the initial explosion at the start of its next turn. To use this effect, you must have Tinker's Tools on your person when crafting the bomb.
3. **Enhanced Powder (Cost: 3d6).** The bomb's explosion radius increases to 10 feet. To use this effect, you must have Alchemist's Supplies on your person when crafting the bomb.
