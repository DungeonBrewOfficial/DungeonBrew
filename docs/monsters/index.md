# Drochnath's Spawn

Drochnath’s spirit is one of hopeless wanting, of endless imprisonment, and desperate longing. The once dormant earth spirit is being smothered by the weight and filth of Kavek’s industrial district crushing its sanctum. Its spawn are fragments of its own form, sloughed off in its desperation to free itself.

## Dreg

Dregs are the rotted, festering bits of Drochnath’s form oozing their way to escape the toxic sludge seeping through the ground that has infected it. The Dreg is driven by a simple, violent impulse to absorb more organic matter into its form and to lash out at any living thing that enters its territory. It does not think or plan; it is an animated, toxic pustule of Drochnath that mindlessly seeks to perpetuate itself.

Dregs are found in the sewers, drainage canals, and refuse pits of Kavesk. Such filth-ridden places are more easily accessible to them, coalescing through grates and the cracks in mortar to feed on vermin, carrion, and any other creature that crosses their path.

## Slough

A Slough is a manifestation of Drochnath's will to unmake the city of Kavesk. It is formed when the Tatarigami forces its own deleterious, semi-liquid body up through the earth, assimilating the rock, brick, and mortar of the city's foundations to create a dense, powerful body of animated sludge and rubble.

The Slough percolates through the under-foundations and subterranean levels of Kavesk's industrial district, trying to seep its way to the surface. Once it finds its way, it is indistinguishable from a pile of debris. It is drawn to the vibrations of the city above, emerging to gnaw at the city’s bones and carry out Drochnath's rage.

# Váttkorr's Spawn

The creation of the Gaospod began with the fall of the Dwergar deity, Váttkorr, guardian of mountain lake of Glisméri. As the Dwergar expanded their industry, the runoff polluted the lake, turning it into a foul cesspool of sludge and waste. This corruption seeped into the surrounding land and waters, infecting any creatures or mortals that came into contact with the pollution. The afflicted were transformed into Gaospod, carriers of the Váttkorr’s curse, spreading his vengeance far beyond the poisoned waters of Glisméri.

## The Cycle of Infection
The Gaospod perpetuate their existence through physical contact: a single scratch introduces the curse into a victim’s body. Over the course of days, the curse consumes the host, warping their body into a monstrous form that reflects Váttkorr’s corrupted reality. Each new Gaospod becomes both a carrier of the curse and an extension of Váttkorr’s wrath.

Though the lower forms of Gaospod are mindless and frenzied, driven only to spread corruption, the most powerful among them retain fragments of their former selves. These higher forms act with purpose, targeting the civilizations and individuals their progenitor believes are responsible for desecrating its sanctum.

## Spawn
A Gaospod Spawn is a humanoid warped by the curse’s initial onset. Its flesh is stretched thin over swollen tissues that weep a black, foul-smelling ichor, and its movements are the jerky, unnatural spasms of a body in revolt. Driven by a feverish, burning agony, a Spawn is a vessel of pain that attacks any living thing without reason or strategy, seeking only to spread the infection that consumes it.

## Ichorling
An Ichorling is a beast that has been fully corrupted by Váttkorr's blight, its form a barely recognizable mockery of its original shape. These creatures are the most basic vectors of the curse, acting as swift, elusive carriers of the physical contamination. They move with a skittering, unnatural speed, still beholden to their basic instincts to hunt and consume.

## Wretch
A Wretch is a humanoid host whose body is malformed with elongated limbs and cracked skin, but it can shapeshift into the guise of its former self, hiding its grotesque nature. It retains fragmented memories of its mortal life, which it uses to psychologically torment its victims before striking.

## Crackback
A Crackback is a hulking engine of destruction, formed when the curse hardens a beasts’s body with a shell of polluted sediment and broken stone. Its heavy, plated form is resilient, almost impervious to external threats. The stench of decay and industrial filth that hangs about it is an overwhelming, toxic fume.

## Snarl
A Snarl is an unstable evolution of the Wretch, its psyche fractured by the conflict between its host's lingering personality and the curse's corruption. This internal war manifests as manic, unsettling laughter and erratic behavior, shifting between cunning strategy and frenzied chaos. A Snarl wreaks havoc, worming its way into the thoughts of its victims.

## Rasper
The Rasper is the apex of the bestial Gaospod metamorphosis that hunts with violent precision. Its long, lean body is that of a predator and only the most feral instincts remain. Solitary, except for when controlled by an Echo, they will attack anything that enters their domain without hesitation. Their sheer ferocity and capacity for destruction make them the ultimate shock troops of the Gaospod, difficult to direct but impossible to ignore.

## Echo
The Echo represents the pinnacle of humanoid Gaospod transformation, standing as towering, slick, oozing creatures. Highly intelligent, manipulative, and exercising strict control over lesser Gaospod, they wreak havoc by impersonating others while silently commanding their kith to spread the curse. Echoes are rarely seen, preferring to use their shapeshifting abilities to remain disguised as they orchestrate large-scale infestations. They carry the full weight of Váttkorr’s hatred and may taunt their foes with intimate knowledge gleaned from the fragmented memories of the countless victims absorbed by the curse.
