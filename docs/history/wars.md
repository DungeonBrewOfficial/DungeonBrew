# Wars of the Blight Age

## ***The Askaran-Bethune Border Wars (Intermittent)***

*Askara vs. Bethune*   
**Context.** A prolonged series of skirmishes and clashes over contested borderlands. Askara relied on guerrilla warfare and mystical traditions, while Bethune utilized black powder to push its industrial expansion.  
**Outcome.** Askara has been steadily pushed back over the centuries, defending its borders but at significant cost. The wars isolated Askara further, solidifying its identity as a resistant yet embattled traditionalist nation.

## ***The Buhang War (222–240 BTA)***

*Gialar  vs. Marcia*   
**Context.** Marcia sought access to Gialar’s rich mountain resources. The Kapre, defending their sacred territories, waged a guerrilla-style resistance using the mountainous terrain to their advantage.  
**Outcome.** While Marcia gained footholds in lower mountain regions, the Kapre retained their most sacred sites. The war deepened animosities and set the stage for future resource-driven conflicts during the Blight Age.

## ***The Fomorian Incursions (486–502 BTA)***

*Khunland vs. Avenar and Ryhoreiral*  
**Context.** Unified under a warlord, Fomorian-led Khunish clans launched raids into Avenar, targeting farmland and settlements. Ryhoreiral supported Avenar militarily, marking a rare display of international cooperation.  
**Outcome.** The allied forces repelled the incursions but failed to eliminate the threat completely. The Fomorians regrouped, maintaining the potential for future conflicts.

## ***The Ten-Years War (535–545 BTA)***

*Bethune vs. Ibunor*   
**Context.** A war between two industrial giants over trade routes and influence on Oskara. Both nations heavily relied on black powder weaponry, with the war marking a shift toward large-scale industrial conflicts.  
**Outcome.** The war ended in a costly stalemate, with both nations suffering heavy losses. The rivalry persisted, leading to economic and diplomatic tensions.

## ***The Second Siege of Kizar Korath (648–660 BA)***

*Korath vs. Baleros*  
**Context.** Baleros launched an invasion of Korath, targeting its southern territories rich in mineral resources. Korath, already weakened by internal divisions, relied on its fortified cities to repel the Cyclopian forces.  
**Outcome.** Korath narrowly repelled Baleros after a grueling 12-year siege. Though victorious, Korath’s infrastructure was severely damaged, while Baleros suffered heavy losses.

## ***The Steinlund Conflict (671 BTA- Ongoing)***

*Bethune  vs. Steinlund*  
**Context.** Bethune attempted to extend its railway through Steinlund, prompting the Zwerge to resist industrial encroachment. The conflict saw increased Zwerge fortifications countering Bethune’s black powder forces.  
**Outcome.** Steinlund halted Bethune’s expansion, maintaining its territorial sovereignty. The conflict remains unresolved, characterized by stalemates and occasional skirmishes.

## ***The Marcian Debt Wars (676 BTA–Ongoing)***

*Marcia vs. Gialar*  
**Context.** Marcia’s industrial ambitions reignited conflict with Gialar, as the Marcian push for critical mountain resources intensified. The Kapre leveraged their knowledge of the terrain to sabotage mining operations and infrastructure.  
**Outcome.** Marcia retained access to some resources but at great cost, with significant mining operations rendered unusable. The war entrenched hostility between the two nations.

## ***Black Powder Development and The Ten-Years War***

393 BTA: Ibunor develops the first firearm, pioneering black powder technology.  
407 BTA: Bethune begins adopting black powder, creating an arms race.  
535 BTA: Ibunor declares war on Bethune, initiating the Ten-Years War.  
538 BTA: Black powder becomes central to Bethune’s military strategy.  
541-542 BTA: Other nations, including the Dwergar of Kazak and Korath, and the humans of Marcia, begin integrating black powder.  
545 BTA: The Ten-Years War ends inconclusively, leaving both empires bloodied but determined.

## ***The Conquest of Gane (Ogre Wars)***

Ibunor waged war against the ogres of Gane for over 300 years before fully subjugating them.

330–350 BTA: Ogre warbands raid Ibunese settlements in the Northern Raids.  
380–410 BTA: The Border Wars begin as Ibunor expands southward.  
450–470 BTA: The First Ibunese Invasion fails due to the Ogres’ endurance tactics.  
500–540 BTA: Ibunor shifts to indirect tactics, fueling tribal infighting.  
570–600 BTA: A War of Attrition weakens Gane through supply-line control.  
622–632 BTA: The Final Conquest subjugates Gane, incorporating ogres into the Ibunese military as shock troops.  
641–648 BTA: The Long March forces Ganic ogres to fight against Keld in brutal northern campaigns.
