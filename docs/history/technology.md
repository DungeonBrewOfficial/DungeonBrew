# Powder and Steam

<div class="page-split-equal" markdown="1">

<div class="sidebar-content" markdown="1">

Dheghom is in the midst of an upheaval as technology takes center stage. The impact of steam power, clockwork, and black powder's rise cannot be reconciled with a single invention or innovation. In fact, innovation itself has always stemmed from desperation, ambition, and greed, causing the fruit of these technologies to be rotten. Or, at least, it has always seemed so among the technologically illiterate and the disenfranchised who have been turned into grist. For those driving that great machine, power, wealth, and the future itself, could not be better.  

This section explores the ways steam power, clockwork, and black powder shape adventures and stories in Dheghom.

---

#### Perspectives on Progress

Attitudes toward technology vary across Dheghom’s nations. In industrialized centers like Bellegrad, factories dominate the landscape, and machines have become a part of daily life. There, the wealthy and powerful drive technological progress, profiting from the blood and sweat of factory workers. Meanwhile, places like Ryhoreiral and Gialar cling to older ways of life, rejecting industrialization to preserve their spiritual and cultural identities. These societies often view technology with suspicion, seeing it as a corrupting influence that prioritizes greed over harmony.

---

#### The Cost of Innovation

Technological advancement in Dheghom comes with steep consequences. Industrialized cities are choked with soot and ash, polluting rivers that flow black with chemical runoff. Extraction of natural resources scars the land, turning forests into barren plains and sacred sites into mining pits. This destruction poisons the earth, breaks spiritual and cultural ties to the land, and leaves communities alienated from their heritage.  

The mortal toll is equally grim. Factories prioritize efficiency over safety, treating workers as living parts in a vast machine. Death, disfigurement, and despair are commonplace among the laboring class, and entire communities are displaced to meet the ever-growing demands of industry. The price for these new technologies is paid in human suffering, exploitation, and loss.

</div>

<div class="main-content" markdown="1">

!!! info "Clockwork"
	Intricate mechanisms of gears, springs, and levers, clockwork devices are valued for precision, automation of simple tasks, or security. They do not involve self-aware automatons. 
	
	<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">
	
	**Primary Uses:** Timing devices (public clocks, personal pocket watches, more reliable fuses for some explosives); complex locking mechanisms for vaults and strongboxes; fine gears in scientific or navigational instruments (chronometers, sextants); some luxury items like music boxes or intricate toys. 
	
	**Impact:** Allows for precise measurement and control in specific applications. Such devices are typically expensive, require skilled artisans (Clock-Wrights) to create and maintain, and can be delicate or prone to jamming in harsh conditions. Access to quality clockwork is often a mark of wealth.
	
	</div>
	
#### Contradictions and Conflicts

The proliferation of technology exacerbates conflict across Dheghom. Nations armed with cannons and rifles gain advantages, pressuring allies and enemies alike to follow suit, or fall behind. Whether driven by necessity or greed, the pressure inevitably grinds those on the lowest rungs into the dirt. As a result, many nations find themselves fighting both external and internal wars as rivals try to take any advantage they can find and factions vie for power.  

Technology has become so ingrained in many nations and cultures that they have begun to lose sight of any identity that does not include it. Meanwhile, traditional nations and cultures continue to rebel against the rise of black powder and steam, choosing to rely on more antiquated means of survival and warfare, oftentimes ostracizing members of their communities that stray too far from the paths of their ancestors. In the end, technology has become a source of tension, conflict, and outright violence regardless of actual adoption.

## Technological Tensions

Technological advancement has reshaped Dheghom, creating divides between nations that embrace progress and those that remain steeped in tradition. These differences fuel rivalries and alliances, as industrialized powers expand their reach and traditionalist nations defend their ways of life. Conflicts over resources, trade, and ideologies often drive political and military tensions, shaping the interactions between nations. Industrial growth demands vast resources. Coal, iron, and timber are essential, pushing nations with factories to seek control over these materials wherever they lie. This often leads to conflict with traditional societies or rival powers over resource rich territories. Simultaneously, new black powder weapons and steam powered machines of war change the nature of conflict. Nations with superior technology gain significant advantages, forcing others into a difficult choice: adapt and industrialize, or face potential domination.

</div>

<div class="main-content" markdown="1">

!!! info "Black Powder"
	An explosive chemical mixture, black powder remains the dominant force in warfare and a crude tool for industry. Dynamite has not been invented, and nitroglycerin is not yet a factor. 
	
	<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">
	
	**Primary Uses:** Propellant for firearms; charges for cannons of all sizes (field, naval, siege); creation of crude bombs (e.g., powder kegs with fuses, basic hand-thrown grenades); rough blasting for mining or basic demolition. 
	
	**Impact:** Has revolutionized warfare, making older tactics and armor increasingly obsolete. Its production is a key strategic industry. It is notoriously dangerous to manufacture, store, and handle, with accidental explosions common.
	
	</div>

#### Advancement Types

**Integrated Industry.** A fully developed industrial economy connected by railways or steamships.  

**Diversified Industry.** A complex industrial base with manufacturing sectors beyond foundational industries.  

**Factory Economy.** The primary mode of production has shifted to factories and a dedicated industrial workforce.  

**Steam Power.** Adoption of steam as a primary power source for its nascent industries.  

**Foundational Power.** The capacity to mass-produce foundational materials, such as iron and coal.  

**Incipient Industry.** Mechanizing a single, key economic sector while the wider economy remains pre-industrial.  

**Borrowed Industry.** Importing modern technologies without the infrastructure to produce them.  

**Pre-Industrial.** An economy is based on manual labor and craft, with no mechanized production.

---

#### Adoption Types

**Innovator.** Developer of new technologies, acting as a primary source of invention.  

**Early Adopter.** Adopter of new technologies after they are introduced to gain a competitive advantage.  

**Early Majority.** Adopter of new technologies only after they have been proven effective by others.  

**Late Majority.** Adopter of new technologies only after it has become commonplace, often due to social pressure or necessity.  

**Laggard.** Resistant to change and is the last to adopt new technologies, if they do so at all.

---

#### Role of Magic

**Integral.** A central organizing principle of the society, inseparable from its culture and survival.  

**Specialized.** A formal discipline practiced and controlled by a specific group of specialists.  

**Marginal.** Exists on the fringes of society as superstition, hedge-magic, or forgotten lore.  

**Supplanted.** Almost entirely replaced by technology and no longer possesses an active societal role.

---

#### Magic Attitude

**Revered.** Seen as holy or essential to a nation's identity and way of life.  

**Pragmatic.** Viewed as a useful tool to be employed when effective, without spiritual attachment.  

**Tolerated.** Acknowledged to exist but met with general indifference or apathy.  

**Regulated.** Actively controlled, licensed, or restricted by a governing authority.  

**Feared.** Seen as a dangerous, corrupting, or unpredictable force to be avoided.  

**Shunned.** Actively suppressed, outlawed, or treated with cultural hostility and shame.

<div class="main-content" markdown="1">

!!! info "Steam Power"
	The true engine of the new age, steam power is generated by heating water, typically with coal, to produce pressurized steam that drives pistons and turbines. 
	
	<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">
	
	**Primary Uses:** Fueling engines in factories for all manner of production; powering locomotives that pull trains across newly laid railways; turning the propellers and paddlewheels of steamships. It's also used for heavy machinery like industrial pumps, lifts, and some experimental equipment.
	
	**Impact:** Enables levels of mass production and resource extraction. It drives demand for coal and iron, leads to the development of extensive rail networks, and results in cities choked with smoke and rivers blackened by industrial runoff. Labor is increasingly centralized in large, often dangerous, factory settings.
	
	</div>

## Technological Adventures

Technology's presence provides unique scenarios for your Dheghom campaign. You might hook the party with a factory poisoning local lands; perhaps they must choose between direct action against the owners and navigating the desperation of workers needing the jobs. Consider adventures aiding or thwarting rebels fighting industrial expansion, or missions to sabotage machines of war. Sites like strip mines, railway yards, and factories can offer a change to traditional dungeon delving, offering a chance to create unfamiliar challenges for your players.  

Beyond quests and locations, technology forces difficult internal struggles upon both PCs and NPCs. Consider an inventor wrestling with the unintended uses of their creations: does pride, profit, or a sliver of conscience guide their actions? For the community resisting industrial encroachment, how do they measure their heritage against the necessity of adopting the technology they despise? 

Exploring these conflicts allows you to delve into the seductive lure of technological power, the compromises forced by survival, and the erosion of principles under constant pressure.

### Personal Impact

Technology has left its mark on every corner of Dheghom, shaping the lives of those who thrive and those who suffer under its weight. Whether through innovation, exploitation, or resistance, no one is untouched by its reach. When players create characters in a Dheghom campaign, consider how technology’s rise has influenced their backgrounds. Here are a few topics to explore:  

<div class="hanging-indent" markdown="1">

**Labor and Industry.** Was the character a laborer in a factory or workshop? Did they witness firsthand the exploitation of workers, the danger of unsafe machinery, or the destruction of their craft as machines replaced artisans? Did they rise to resist their conditions, or did they comply to survive?  

**Rural Upheaval.** If the character hails from a rural area, how has industrial expansion affected their home? Was their village displaced to make way for a railway or factory? Did they grow up hearing tales of the land’s desecration or seeing the scars of logging and mining operations?  

**Technological Advancement.** Does the character embrace progress? Were they an engineer or inventor, someone who saw the potential of steam power, clockwork, or black powder as a means to change the world? Did they create a device or weapon that fell into the wrong hands, and do they now seek to atone?  

**Traditional Beliefs.** Did the character come from a community or culture that rejects industrialization? Were they raised to view technology as a corruption of natural or divine order? If so, do they still hold these beliefs, or have they been swayed by the power of progress?  

**Personal Loss.** What has the character lost to this age? Did their family suffer from exploitation, displacement, or environmental destruction? Were they separated from their loved ones by economic pressures, forced to migrate to cities or mines?  

</div>

### Everyday Impact

There are few corners of Dheghom untouched by the creeping spread of industrialization. Even most isolated villages feel its shadow through distant smoke trails of factory stacks or the roar of trains carving paths across once-untouched landscapes. For those in cities, the Age of Steam has brought upheaval, displacing artisans, exploiting laborers, and reshaping lives. Any time you’re setting a scene, consider how steam power, black powder, and industrial ambition impact the world around the characters. 

The Everyday Impact table offers suggestions for weaving these themes into daily life and adding depth to your settings.
