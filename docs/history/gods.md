# The Gods

*Supreme deities and lesser gods of Dheghom, their manifestations, sanctums, and associated cultures.*

## **Supreme Deities**

Most cultures recognize a primary deity for their particular pantheon. However, a given pantheon is not necessarily interrelated, nor do the deities within a pantheon necessarily separate their provinces or domains as one might expect. As a result, it is not uncommon for some cultures to have multiple war or life deities in addition to several deities that preside over water, land, or sky. The origins of Dheghom and the plethora of deific beings permits a unique and diverse cast of gods to preside over a region.

#### ***An Banrigh / An Bandia (The Queen / The Goddess)***

**Status.** Absent.  
**Summary.** An Banrigh is the Daoine Sí deity of peace and life, who ruled their people directly before vanishing sometime during the Blood Age.  
**Physical Manifestation.** She has not been seen in generations, but  is described in ancient texts as a woman whose form was that of a feminine tree and whose hair was a cascade of living flora.  
**Sanctum.** Ard-Suí, the high throne room within the city of Killan.  
**Associated Culture.** The Daoine Sí of Brynnalyn.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Her worship is fractured. The Maors of An Dáil perform the ancient, formal rituals before her empty throne. The followers of An Fáidh practice newer rites based on the Seer's visions, often involving acts of extreme sacrifice intended to compel her return.  
**Known Symbols.** The Lus an Airgid (Silver Tear blossom).  
**Known Behavior.** Absent.

#### ***Bryshteya: She of the Shifting Waters***

**Status**. Active.  
**Summary.** Bryshteya is the Marcian deity of the Morye Tyshi Sea, associated with storms and the mysteries of the deep. She is believed to have once swallowed the moon to hide it from invading fleets, and her breath is said to form the fog banks that shroud the coasts.  
**Physical Manifestation.** She appears as a massive leviathan with deep blue-green scales.  
**Sanctum.** The Morye Tyshi Sea.  
**Associated Culture.** The Marcians of Kavesk, particularly sailors and fisherfolk.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Marcian priests inscribe ancient runes on driftwood and send it adrift during storms to communicate with the deity. The primary sacred site is Brystholm, where sailors and fisherfolk cast polished stones into the sea as offerings to seek safe passage. As Marcian coastal territories face increased conflict, her worship has become a focal point for unity.  
**Known Symbols.** A wave cresting into the shape of an eye.  
**Known Behavior.** Rarely seen except during storms.

#### ***Chaturmukha: The Four-Faced One***

**Status**. Active.  
**Summary.** Chaturmukha is the Askaran deity of law and order. He presides over the justice that the Askarans believe was first written in the stars.  
**Physical Manifestation.** He appears as a four-faced giant composed of black marble.  
**Sanctum.** Jarah Peak, a mountain in the Charan range.  
**Associated Culture.** The tribes of Askara.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The priests of the Vidhimandir, his largest temple, oversee his rites. This temple is in the city of Jarah and contains a large, rotating bronze idol with mirrors.  
**Known Symbols.** A cube depicting an eye on four of its sides.  
**Known Behavior.** His presence allows the city of Jarah to function as neutral ground for diplomacy among the tribes.

#### ***Coedryn: The Great Elk of Draenwydd***

**Status.** Active.  
**Summary.** Coedryn is the Ellyllon deity of nature and knowledge.  
**Physical Manifestation.** He appears as a great elk with bark-like hide and antlers twisted with thorns.  
**Sanctum.** Draenwydd, a forest of massive trees at the heart of Tytherin.  
**Associated Culture.** The Ellyllon of Tytherin. Among them, The Tavs oversee the rites of the gods, and for Coedryn, Porthbwyd guide Tav young hunters through their first hunt.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Derwriad are the faction that tends to the gods. Tavs within this order act as priests and guardians who maintain sacred sites, oversee offerings, and pass down oral histories.  
**Known Symbols.** A thorned antler circled by roots.  
**Known Behavior.** He is rarely seen directly, but his silhouette is sometimes glimpsed moving between the giant trees at dawn or dusk.

#### ***Gialar: The Great Vulture***

**Status.** Active.  
**Summary.** Gialar is the Kapre deity symbolizing the cycle of life and death. He is believed to be the Great Vulture who gathers what is left from the fallen, ensuring that what is taken feeds new life and what remains feeds eternity.   
**Physical Manifestation.** He appears as a great vulture that soars over his province.   
**Sanctum.** Kallunga, a city carved into the sandstone cliffs of Gialar.  
**Associated Culture.** The Kapre.   
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Kapre priesthood makes offerings of relics and sacred artifacts connected to the cycle of life and death. Their rituals emphasize respect for balance and renewal.  
**Known Symbols.** The Eye of Gialar. This is a composite symbol formed from the images of a sun, a crescent moon, and a serpent.   
**Known Behavior.** He is known to soar in the skies above his province.

#### ***Grimram: The Iron-Horned Ram***

**Status.** Active.  
**Summary.** Grimram is the Dwergar deity of crafting and order. He is believed to have shaped the first Dwergar halls by breaking open the mountains, and wild mountain rams are believed to act as his eyes.  
**Physical Manifestation.** He appears as a colossal mountain ram with horns of polished iron and a hide of coarse stone streaked with metallic veins.  
**Sanctum.** Grimhold, a spiraling mountain pass in the Brotin Halm Fells, composed of ironstones.  
**Associated Culture.** The Dwergar, primarily miners and smiths with shrines in the six forges of Korath and larger temples in Alms, The Toll, and Temple Heights.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Dwergar smiths and priests observe the flow of slag to divine Grimram's will from the patterns. Miners leave offerings of small, carved rams at new mineral veins. His name is invoked more often as the Gaospod crisis increases.  
**Known Symbols.** Small carvings of rams used as talismans and offerings.  
**Known Behavior.** He is seen wandering his sanctum, the spiraling mountain pass of Grimhold. 

#### ***Hotaru-no-kōgō: The Gilded Empress***

**Status.** Tatarigami (Corrupted).  
**Summary.** The empress-goddess of the Oni, once revered as the living embodiment of perfection. An assassination attempt by the Shugo failed, wounding her and triggering her transformation into a Tatarigami.  
**Physical Manifestation.** Originally a being of immaculate ivory. She is now a figure of obsidian held together by veins of molten gold.  
**Sanctum.** Komorebi (The Sunlit Grove), now a fortified temple-city and the Morito capital.  
**Associated Culture.** The Morito of Kimara.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Her worship is the foundation of the Morito faction, a militarized monastic state that venerates her transformed state as a unique form of broken beauty and seeks to protect her and all other deities.  
**Known Symbols.** A fractured ivory mask veined with gold.  
**Known Behavior.** He is seen wandering his sanctum, the spiraling mountain pass of Grimhold. 

#### ***Khazraya: The Maker and Mender***

**Status.** Active.  
**Summary.** Khazraya is the Zwerge deity of labor and endurance. She presides over the forges and the rites of binding that allow the dwindling Zwerge of Steinlund to persist through undeath.  
**Physical Manifestation.** She appears as an immense badger. Her fur is scarred, and her eyes glow like molten stone.  
**Sanctum.** Her sanctum is Urkhaskraal, the great necropolis deep beneath the mountains of Steinlund. It serves as both a city for the living and the dead.  
**Known Symbols.** A fractured stone wheel.  
**Associated Culture.** The Zwerge of Steinlund. Their faith is one of silent toil rather than overt worship.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Stone Voices are priests who oversee the rites of binding. In these rites, performed in Khazraya's name, the dead are returned to their labor. This practice is central to the Zwerge's survival and identity.  
**Known Behavior.** Khazraya's primary interaction with the Zwerge is the enactment of the rite of binding. The strain of this ongoing duty is beginning to show; some among the Stone Voices whisper that the forge-fires in Urkhaskraal now burn lower and that the rites seem to take more from her than they once did.

#### ***Morríon: The Bog-Mother***

**Status**. Active.  
**Summary.** Morríg is the Aos Sí deity of cyclical life, decay, and rebirth. Her sanctum, the great bog of Mona Rua, is seen by the Aos Sí as the heart of their land and the source of all life.  
**Physical Manifestation.** She appears as a massive, serpentine creature with the upper torso of a woman. Her lower body is a long, powerful serpent's tail the color of dark, wet earth, and her skin is covered in iridescent, scale-like patterns.  
**Sanctum.** The Mona Rua, a vast tropical bog in the heart of Ryhoreiral.  
**Known Symbols.** A serpent coiled around a blooming water lily.  
**Associated Culture.** The Aos Sí.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Lacking a formal priesthood, the most respected elders of the kin-groups are considered her spiritual conduits. Rites involve the ceremonial burial of offerings in the bog entrusting them to the goddess's cycle of preservation and decay.  
**Known Behavior.** She is a reclusive and silent deity. The Aos Sí believe her silence is not one of absence, but of profound, ancient contemplation, and they do not seek to disturb it with frivolous prayers.

#### ***Mowes Kas: The Swan Maiden***

**Status**. Tatarigami (Corrupted).  
**Summary.** Mowes Kas is a Tatarigami who was originally an Ibunese goddess known as Itxaro. In her swan form, she was shot through the heart with an arrow during the Blood Age. After fleeing north, she was discovered in her humanoid form by the Powrie, who gave her sanctuary. Her divine anger from this unhealed wound now manifests as a corrupting madness that infects her followers and drives their holy war.  
**Physical Manifestation.** She appears as a beautiful woman with large, white swan wings. An ancient arrow remains permanently embedded in her chest. The wound around it is packed and bound by her followers but never heals.  
**Sanctum.** A lost lake in Ibunor's Itzalburu Basoa Wood.  
**Known Symbols.** A heart pierced by an arrow.  
**Associated Culture.** The Powrie clans of Keld.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Medhygyon continuously tend to the goddess's wound and convey her spoken will. Her powerful emotions emanate from her as a madness that infects her followers.  
**Known Behavior.** She is an articulate deity who expresses both a fierce, protective love for the Powrie who saved her and a burning hatred for the outside world that wounded her. She is not a prisoner, but her wound tethers her to her followers, whose rage she directs as a weapon.

#### ***Orophylax: The Imprisoned God***

**Status**. Unknown.  
**Summary.** Orophylax is a Tatarigami and the central deity of the Cyclopian faith in Baleros.  Baleran tradition holds that Orophylax is entombed within its sanctum.   
**Physical Manifestation.** He appears as a dragon whose obsidian scales are scarred and broken. Veins of magma are visible in the cracks of its hide.  
**Sanctum.** Grjótvirki with the Ori Balarika mountains.  
**Associated Culture.** The Cyclopians of Baleros.  For many, their faith and cultural identity are centered on the multi-millennial war with Korath to liberate their god and its sanctum.   
**Priesthood & Rites.** Worship is highly organized and overseen by a state priesthood intertwined with the military.  State rituals emphasize remembrance of the "Great Betrayal," veneration of their imprisoned god, and the pursuit of his freedom. Common acts of devotion include pilgrimages toward the border of the occupied mountains.   
**Known Symbols.** A single, jagged dragon's tooth that appears as a mountain peak when inverted.  
**Known Behavior.** Orophylax has not been seen by Balerans in millennia. The volcanic activity in the Ori Balarika mountains is interpreted by the Balerans as the physical manifestation of its torment and righteous anger at its captors.

#### ***Papa Fango: The Heart of the Mire***

**Status**. Tatarigami (Corrupted).  
**Summary.** Papa Fango is a Tatarigami, corrupted during the invasion of Acadiana by the Marcians, who wounded him. Historically, he was associated with wisdom and time.  
**Physical Manifestation.** A colossal alligator, its hide caked with mud and overgrown with swamp flora.  
**Sanctum.** The Gran Marè, a swamp now enclosed by a system of dams and channels.   
**Associated Culture.** The Acadians.   
**Priesthood & Rites.** The La Sosyete Mason maintains the sluice gates and dams that keep Papa Fango contained within its swamp.  
**Known Symbols.** A half-submerged jaw.  
**Known Behavior.** Papa Fango rages within its sanctum, hunting anything that comes near.

#### ***Rochaut: The Bull of Gane***

**Status**. Active.  
**Summary.** Rochaut is the Ogre deity of war and forgecraft. He is believed to have carved the valleys of Gane with his horns to create paths for the Ogre tribes.  
**Physical Manifestation.** He appears as an enormous bull with jagged horns. His hide appears to be composed of slate with rust colored veins running throughout.  
**Sanctum.** Brisclat, a high plateau in the lands of Gane.  
**Associated Culture.** The Ogre tribes of Gane. While each tribe typically honors a local god, warriors from many tribes invoke Rochaut for strength. Some find practicing their faith difficult under Ibunese rule.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Ogres honor their gods through action rather than prayer. Warriors carve Rochaut's symbol into their weapons and call upon his strength through ritual before battle. As conflict between tribes escalates, his worship has become central to Ogre identity.  
**Known Symbols.** Twin Horns. The horns and hides of bulls are also used as symbols of strength and protection.  
**Known Behavior.** It is said that Rochaut is sleeping in his sanctum, waiting to be called to war again.

#### ***Shizuko: The Weeping Child***

**Status**. Tatarigami (Corrupted).  
**Summary.** Shizuko was corrupted into a Tatarigami when a faction of the Tangaku school, in an attempt to unlock the secrets of its eternal youth, ambushed and dismembered the god.  
**Physical Manifestation.** Shizuko appears as a young, androgynous child with plain features, who moves without sound, leaves no tracks, skin is always dry, regardless of the weather, and eyes are flat, black surfaces. As a result of the Tengu assault, Shizuko has a weeping stump where its hand once was.  
**Sanctum.** The Shikomori Wood.  
**Associated Culture.** The Tengu of Sharasa.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Shizuko was never worshipped with formal rites, but was the subject of intense, obsessive study by all four of the Ryūha. The Tengu would visit its sanctum to ask it questions and to simply observe the immortal being.  
**Known Symbols.** A birch leaf.  
**Known Behavior.** It can often be found weeping, mutilating the creatures of its sanctum to mirror its own disfigurement.

#### ***Sruth-Tarbh: The River-Bull***

**Status**. Active.   
**Summary.** Sruth-Tarbh is a colossal hippopotamus, believed by the Fomorians to have carved the deep riverbeds with its tusks at the dawn of the world.  
**Physical Manifestation.** It appears as an enormous hippopotamus with a hide like scarred, wet stone and prominent, dagger-like tusks.  
**Sanctum.** Abhainn Corc, a deep and murky section of a major river.  
**Associated Culture.** The Fomorian clans of the riverlands.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Draíodóir lead rites venerating the deity as the shaper of their lands. Offerings of carved stones and grain are cast into its sanctum as a sign of respect and a plea for the river's continued benevolence.  
**Known Symbols.** A crescent-shaped tusk.  
**Known Behavior.** The deity is largely silent and passive, spending long periods submerged. The Fomorians believe its slow movements are the cause of the river's ever-shifting currents and sandbars.

#### ***The God King: Ruler of Bethune***

**Status**. Tatarigami (Corrupted).  
**Summary.** The God King is the absolute ruler of Bethune and a Tatarigami paradoxically associated with life and order. He is revered by his people as a divine protector who rose to power during The Great Plague.  
**Physical Manifestation.** He appears as a larger-than-human figure clad in radiant golden armor, embodying an unsettling and overwhelming presence.  
**Sanctum.** The Kyrie, the ruling district of the city of Bellegrad.  
**Associated Culture.** The people of Bethune, who live under a theocratic monarchy.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Divine Council oversees governance and maintains religious doctrine. Worship is expressed through weekly services in grand cathedrals and festivals glorifying his divine interventions.   
**Known Symbols.** A Golden Helm.  
**Known Behavior.** The God King is rarely seen in public, spending his time hidden in the Kyrie.

#### ***Veden Emo: Mother of Water***

**Status**. Active.   
**Summary.** Veden Emo is seen as a deity of both consistency and change, believed to represent steadfastness and the ability to adapt to the storms of the world.  
**Physical Manifestation.** She appears has the lower body of an ancient, gnarled willow tree rooted to a riverbank, that forms into the upper torso of a woman. Large limbs form her arms with a cascade of branches forming her hair.  
**Sanctum.** A grove on the banks of the Woldwater River.  
**Associated Culture.** The Tonttu.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Veden Emo has no formal priesthood. Tonttu tradition involves leaving offerings of carved stones or hand-woven baskets at the river's edge or merely coming to sit in her grove, listening to the song of her leaves in the wind.  
**Known Symbols.** The willow leaf.  
**Known Behavior.** Veden Emo is an active cultivator of her domain, a lush garden of plantlife springing from her grove near the river.

#### ***Wuduwāsa: The Wounded Guardian***

**Status**: Tatarigami (Corrupted).   
**Summary.** The guardian of Grimholt Wood who was corrupted into a Tatarigami during the Blood Age when it fought alongside the Kazaki Dwergar against the Ogre warbands of Gane.  
**Physical Manifestation.** A colossal, vaguely humanoid figure formed from the living wood, peat, and stone. Its body is scarred and broken, with splintered limbs and a great, weeping fissure in its chest from which a dark, sap-like substance constantly drips.  
**Sanctum.** The Grimholt Wood.  
**Associated Culture.** The Kazaki Dwergar  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Kazaki make pilgrimages to the edge of Wuduwāsa's sanctum to leave offerings, revering him as a wounded veteran of the Ganic Wars.  
**Known Symbols.** A broken branch.  
**Known Behavior.** It wanders its sanctum, often heard weeping and restless. When roused by the sounds of battle or the encroachment of outsiders, its sorrow turns to a blind, destructive rage, lashing out at any living thing it can find.

#### ***Ysgleirio: The Storm-Shepherd***

**Status**: Active.   
**Summary.** Ysgleirio \[us-GLAY-ree-o\] is a giant roc that dwells in the central mountains of Avenar. The Avenarans view it as the harbinger of storms.  
**Physical Manifestation.** It appears as a colossal bird of prey with black, gray, and white feathers and a beak and talons that gleam like polished jet.  
**Sanctum.** Copa'r Cawr \[KOH-pa'r KOWR\], The Giant’s Summit.  
**Associated Culture.** Avenarians.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Ceidwad perform rites at the Fomorian Megaliths at the base of Copa'r Cawr to ask for rain during the dry season and to ask for reprieve during the wet season.  
**Known Symbols.** A black cloud.  
**Known Behavior.** It spends its days circling on the high thermal winds, a distant but ever-present fixture in the Avenarian sky.

## **Lesser Deities**

Most cultures recognize a primary deity for their particular pantheon. However, a given pantheon is not necessarily interrelated, nor do the deities within a pantheon necessarily separate their provinces or domains as one might expect. As a result, it is not uncommon for some cultures to have multiple war or life deities in addition to several deities that preside over water, land, or sky. The origins of Dheghom and the plethora of deific beings permits a unique and diverse cast of gods to preside over a region.

#### ***Alainn: The Lady without Shadow***

**Status**: Tatarigami (Corrupted).   
**Summary.** Alainn was the revered deity of Cathair Lir. She was a fey being of grace and light who was corrupted into a Tatarigami during the magical cataclysm that destroyed the city.  
**Physical Manifestation.** She appears as a tall, unnaturally beautiful woman of a lineage unknown to Dheghom. Though she seems to be a being of flesh and blood who casts no shadow, and her face is always perfectly and evenly lit, regardless of the surrounding light.  
**Sanctum.** Her sanctum was An Grianán (The Sunny Place), a great, sunlit garden at the heart of Cathair Lir.  
**Associated Culture.** The Aos Sí of Cathair Lir (formerly).  
**Priesthood & Rites.** She was not worshipped with formal rites but was a revered presence. The Aos Sí would seek out her sanctum for quiet contemplation, believing her presence inspired serenity and creativity.  
**Known Symbols.** A white ring.  
**Known Behavior.** Her original behavior was passive and benevolent. She would wander her sanctum, a silent and calming figure.

#### ***Argant: The Shattered Echo***

**Status**: Tatarigami (Corrupted).   
**Summary.** Argant was a Griffon god whose followers were annihilated during the Powrie civil war. The trauma of the event shattered his mind, corrupting him into a Tatarigami. He is now trapped in a perpetual, looping re-enactment of his people’s final, bloody moments.  
**Physical Manifestation.** Argant manifests as a massive Griffon.   
**Sanctum.** Wyrkmeer Lake.  
**Associated Culture.** The Gwerin Argant, a loose, desperate band of Powrie outcasts and scavengers.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Gwerin Argant do not revere the god but instead practice a form of high-risk scavenging, dodging his violent outbursts, and looting the tangible results of his episodes.  
**Known Symbols.** A griffon's feather.  
**Known Behavior.** Argant is trapped in a constant cycle of re-living his final battle, attacking anyone he perceives as a threat. He will also instinctively hunt and kill local wildlife, leaving the carcasses as offerings for his phantom clan.

#### ***Arlothanoth: The Deep Wyrm***

**Status**: Active.   
**Summary.** Arlothanoth is a monolithic force of the deep earth, discovered by a lost clan of Powrie who fled underground during the civil war.  
**Physical Manifestation.** Arlothanoth appears as a massive, ancient Lindworm, whose scales have the color and texture of cooled magma.  
**Sanctum.** The Klyfts.  
**Associated Culture.** The Gwerin Argant, a loose, desperate band of Powrie outcasts and scavengers.  
**Priesthood & Rites.** The Gwerin Arlothanoth, a clan of Powrie who have been physically and culturally altered by centuries of subterranean life.  
**Known Symbols.** A volcano.  
**Known Behavior.** Arlothanoth is a force of nature, like a tectonic plate or a volcanic vent. His body gives off immense geothermal heat, and the rhythmic cycles of his activity are the "days" and "seasons" by which his followers measure their existence.

#### ***Bodach na Coille: The Old Man of the Wood***

**Status**: Tatarigami (Corrupted).   
**Summary.** This entity was once an ancient guardian of the forest, revered by Daoine Sí, but became lost to the magical blight that covers the island.  
**Physical Manifestation.** Appears as a massive, gnarled treant whose bark is a sickly, peeling yellow, like old parchment. Leafless, its twisted branches weep a black, sticky sap that poisons the ground.  
**Sanctum.** A petrified, blighted forest on Brynnalyn.  
**Associated Culture.** The Daoine Sí of Brynnalyn (formerly).  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Once revered as a spirit of the forest, all formal worship has ceased. Now, he is a figure of terror in local folklore.  
**Known Symbols.** A twisted branch.  
**Known Behavior.** It wanders its sanctum, corrupting the vegetation into manifestations of anger and disgust, hunting any living thing that enters its domain.

#### ***Brontotheris: The Broken Thunder***

**Status**: Tatarigami (Corrupted).   
**Summary.** Once a Fomorian protector-god of the western wilds, Brontotheris is a Tatarigami consumed by grief and failure. It was corrupted after its Fomorian followers were massacred by the invading Avenarans.  
**Physical Manifestation.** It appears as a massive V-horned megacerops whose hide is scarred and broken, resembling fractured stone plates. A constant, low rumble of sorrow emanates from its chest.  
**Sanctum.** The Tir na Gaoth Wilds, a windswept expanse of coastal hills and grasslands.  
**Associated Culture.** The Avenarians and Fomorians (formerly).  
**Priesthood & Rites.** Avenarians attempt to leave offerings and sate the madness of the god through rituals on the edges of its sanctum. It is considered a good omen to have Brontotheris see you but not charge.  
**Known Symbols.** A V-shaped horn.  
**Known Behavior.** It wanders the wilds in a state of unrest. Generally unpredictable, it will sometimes merely be wandering, charge anything that moves, attempt to destroy the land, and even has been known to consume creatures it runs down. 

#### ***Bwa Tòde: The Tide-Watcher***

**Status**: Tatarigami (Corrupted).
