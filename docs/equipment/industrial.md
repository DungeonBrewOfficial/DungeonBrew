# Industrial Tools & Weapons

## Power Sources

### Kinetic Harness

*Power Source, Clockwork*

A kinetic harness acts as a power source for industrial and military equipment and can always power one primary Attachment, which often have their own modules. In addition, more powerful harnesses can power additional accessories, which increase the versatility of the wearer.

**Attachment Bonus.** The attachment bonus operates as an Enhancement Bonus, which can be divided between Attachments that are connected to the harness. The amount of power your Attachments receive can be adjusted as a part of a Short or Long Rest.

**Accessories.** Accessories are slots that allow the wearer to utilize other types of equipment that are powered from the harness. These do not draw from the Attachment Bonus and only function so long as there are enough accessory slots available. The specific accessories can be adjusted as a part of a Short or Long Rest.

**Enhancement Bonus.** The value of the Attachment Bonus can be divided among attached weapons and armor until all attachment bonus values have been accounted for.

- Some Attachments have a set value that cannot be changed.

### Boiler Backpack

*Power Source, Steam Power*

A boiler backpack acts as a power source for industrial and military equipment and can always power one primary Attachment, which often have their own modules. In addition, more powerful boiler backpacks can power additional accessories, which increase the versatility of the wearer.

**Attachment Bonus.** The attachment bonus operates as an Enhancement Bonus, which can be divided between Attachments that are connected to the backpack. The amount of power your Attachments receive can be adjusted as a part of a Short or Long Rest.

**Accessories.** Accessories are slots that allow the wearer to utilize other types of equipment that are powered from the backpack. These do not draw from the Attachment Bonus and only function so long as there are enough accessory slots available. The specific accessories can be adjusted as a part of a Short or Long Rest.

**Overcharge.** As a Bonus Action, you can redline your backpack to grant a bonus to your next weapon attack with a weapon connected to your backpack up to the number of accessories you can attach to your backpack (1-5). Roll 1d20 and if you roll a value equal to or less than the bonus, your backpack ruptures, and you must take a number of d10s of fire damage equal to the bonus used.

**Enhancement Bonus.** The value of the Attachment Bonus can be divided among attached weapons and armor until all attachment bonus values have been accounted for.

- Some Attachments have a set value that cannot be changed.

### Arc-Light Projector

*Power Source Accessory, Masterwork (Requires Power Source)*

| | |
|:---|:---|
| **Type** | Power Source Accessory |
| **Subtype** | Prism |
| **Rarity** | Masterwork (Requires Power Source) |
| **Cost** | 4,000 GP |
| **Weight** | 5 |

This device consists of a handheld projector unit with two gimbal-mounted carbon-rod electrodes, connected via a thick, insulated cable to an active power source. The power is required to generate the continuous electrical charge for the arc. Each pair of carbon-rod electrodes have 10 charges.

You can take a Utilize action to cause one of the following effects:

**Light.** The projector sheds Bright Light in a 30-foot radius and Dim Light for an additional 30 feet. This effect drains 1 charge for every 10 minutes and lasts until you take a Bonus Action to cycle the power off or until you use another function of the gem.

**Flash.** You expend 1 charge and cause the projector to fire a brilliant beam of light at one creature you can see within 60 feet of yourself. The creature must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be Blinded for 1 minute. The creature repeats the save at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success.

**Burst.** You expend 10 charges and cause the gem to flare with intense light in a 30-foot Cone. Each creature in the Cone makes a saving throw as if struck by the beam created with the Flash action.

Replacing the rods is a Utilize action and consumes a 50 GP rod-pair.
