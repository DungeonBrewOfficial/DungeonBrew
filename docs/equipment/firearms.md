# Firearms

<div class="page-split-reversed" markdown="1">

<div class="sidebar-content" markdown="1">


Firearms represent an increasingly common technology in Dheghom, supplanting traditional arms in both warfare and personal defense. The proliferation of black powder has led to a wide array of weapons, ranging from small, concealable single-shot pistols to large, multi-barreled guns designed for military use. While effective, this technology is far from perfect.

Firearms in Dheghom are often crudely manufactured and inherently unreliable; their mechanisms are prone to jamming and their barrels to fouling, creating a significant risk of malfunction in the field. This introduces a level of calculated risk to their use, as even a well-maintained weapon can fail at a critical moment. The subsequent sections will detail the specific weapons available, along with the mechanical systems that govern their function and potential for failure.

___

## Firearm Grade

Firearms are rated based on quality, from Substandard Grade to Standard Grade to Superior Grade. These grades affect the chance for a weapon to misfire and the cost, as in the Firearm Grade table.

**Substandard Grade.** These firearms are mass-produced with cheap materials and little quality control. They are the common tools of conscripts, bandits, and anyone needing affordable firepower. While functional, they are notoriously crude and prone to failure.

**Standard Grade.** The standard for professional use, these weapons are crafted for military use for increased reliability and standardization. However, they are still products of an imperfect technology and carry an inherent risk.

**Superior Grade.** The pinnacle of firearm technology, each weapon is crafted by a master gunsmith. Built from the finest materials, these firearms are exceptionally reliable. Their extreme rarity and cost make them the signature of the wealthy elite and legendary gunslingers.

**Bola.** On a successful Attack roll, a bola wraps around the legs of a target Grappling it. To escape, the target or a creature within 5 feet of it must take an Action to make a DC 10 Strength (Athletics) check, freeing the Grappled creature on a success. **Gunpowder (horn).** Gunpowder is needed to fire any firearm. A standard horn carries enough powder to fire 40 slugs, or 10 scatter charges. While generally safe, if intentionally packed and ignited, it can create a small 5' explosion.

**Gunpowder (keg).** Standard bulk storage of gunpowder is in small kegs. Each is enough to refill 10 horns. Will explode in a 20 foot area if left in open flame, or intentionally ignited.

## Misfire

Misfire is equal to Base Misfire + 1 for each additional barrel fired as per Multi-Shot Weapon Mastery. When this number or lower is rolled on a D20 as part of an Attack, the gun jams.

**Jam.** A jammed firearm cannot be used to make attacks. As a Utility Action you may attempt to clear the jam using Tinker's Tools (Dexterity or Intelligence). The DC for this check is DC 10 + Misfire. *Success*: The jam is cleared and the gun may be used normally. *Failure:* The gun remains jammed.

**Misfire Table.** In lieu of the Jam mechanic, your GM may instruct you to roll on the Misfire Table below to determine the effect of the misfire.

## Properties

Here are definitions of the properties in the Properties column of the Weapons table.

### Loud

Guns and explosives are not subtle devices and can be easily heard up to 300 feet away.

### Long-Load (x)

Multiple barrels, bolt magazines, etc. A weapon with the Long-Load property can shoot a number of times up to (x) before needing to be reloaded. Reloading a Long-Load weapon takes an Utility Action.

### Special

#### Simple Melee Weapons

**Brass Knuckles.** A set of metal rings worn over the knuckles to add weight and force to a punch.

- **Special:** Add damage to unarmed strikes.

#### Simple Ranged Weapons

**Cane Pistol.** A single-shot pistol with its barrel and firing mechanism concealed within the shaft of a walking cane.

- **Special:** Functions as a Muff Pistol and requires an Intelligence (Investigation) DC 15 check to reveal the pistol. Proficiency with a Cane and Muff Pistol applies.

**Muff Pistol.** A very small, single-shot pistol designed for maximum concealability.

- **Special:** Advantage on Sleight of Hand checks made to conceal this weapon. Being within 5 feet of an enemy doesn't impose Disadvantage on your attack rolls with this firearm if the creature is Surprised.

**Pepperbox.** A pistol featuring a revolving four-barrel cylinder that is manually rotated to align a pre-loaded barrel with the firing mechanism.

- **Special:** A Misfire renders only the current barrel inoperable, allowing subsequent attacks to be made with the remaining loaded barrels.

#### Martial Melee Weapons

**Cane Sword.** A fashionable walking stick that acts as a scabbard, concealing a rapier blade.

- **Special:** Functions as a Rapier and requires an Intelligence (Investigation) DC 15 check to reveal the blade. Proficiency with a Cane and Rapier applies.

#### Martial Ranged Weapons

**Pepperbox Carbine.** A rifle featuring a revolving four-barrel cylinder that is manually rotated to align a pre-loaded barrel with the firing mechanism.

- **Special:** A Misfire renders only the current barrel inoperable, allowing subsequent attacks to be made with the remaining loaded barrels.

**Nock Gun.** A heavy, seven-barreled firearm. It uses a series of selector switches that allow a single trigger pull to ignite a chosen number of barrels, from a single shot up to a simultaneous seven-barrel volley.

- **Special:** The Nock Gun always requires a Utility Action to reload.

## Mastery Properties

Each weapon has a mastery property, which is usable only by a character who has a feature, such as Weapon Mastery, that unlocks the property for the character. The properties are defined below.

### Deadly

If one of the weapon damage dice for an attack rolls its maximum value, you get to roll another dice of the same size and add it to the weapon damage.

### Multi-Shot

Multi barrelled guns are designed with triggers or hammers for each barrel. You can choose to fire one or more barrels as part of a single attack when you use an Action, a Bonus Action, or a Reaction to fire it. Each additional barrel fired beyond the first increases the weapon's misfire range by 1 (e.g., firing four barrels causes misfires on rolls of 4 or lower). Emptying all barrels may limit the number of attacks you can normally make.

### Quick Draw

When this weapon is loaded at the start of combat, as a Reaction, you can make a ranged attack roll against a creature within normal range at disadvantage. If more than one creature is attempting to use this mastery, the highest initiative resolves first.

### Ricochet

If your target is benefiting from cover, but is within 10 feet of a visible surface that is also within range of your weapon, you may make a ranged weapon attack roll, ignoring half or three-quarters cover. If the target has total cover, you may make your weapon attack roll at disadvantage.

### Scatter

When you hit a creature with a ranged attack roll using this weapon, up to two other creatures of your choice within 5 feet of the original target take piercing damage equal to your Dexterity modifier. You can use this property only once per turn.
